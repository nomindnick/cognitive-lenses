## 1. Argument map

### Convergent (multiple independent lenses agree)

- Retail volumetric water charges are the paradigm property-related fee under *Bighorn-Desert View Water Agency v. Verjil* (unverified); the district's "not a property-related fee" fallback is weak-to-foreclosed and should not be its lead argument. [delphi, dialectic, premortem, steelman, tetlock]
- The rate study's own "derived in part from conservation-incentive pricing" language maps onto the defect condemned in *Capistrano* (unverified) and functions as an admission against interest — the single most damaging fact for the district. [delphi, dialectic, premortem, tetlock]
- The 55% rebate tranche, with the district's concession that rebates "mostly benefit customers other than those paying," is an analytically independent violation theory under §6(b)(1)–(3) — a revenue-*use* defect distinct from the rate-*derivation* defect, giving plaintiffs two largely independent paths to a liability finding. [delphi, dialectic, premortem, tetlock]
- The documented 1.6× marginal-cost differential is a genuine cost anchor that distinguishes this record from *Capistrano*'s and pushes outcomes toward severance/partial invalidation/remand rather than wholesale invalidation. [delphi, dialectic, steelman, tetlock, premortem]
- §6(b)(5) puts the burden of proving compliance on the agency; combined with the district's on-the-record concessions, this converts the case from a neutral methodology dispute into one where the district litigates against its own documents. [delphi, dialectic, tetlock, machinery, steelman]
- The unquantified dollar gap between $0.85/hcf and what the 1.6× differential strictly justifies is the single largest outcome-determinative unknown; obtaining the base Tier 2 rate and rate-study workpapers is the top evidentiary task for either side. [delphi, dialectic, premortem, steelman, tetlock]
- Art. X, §2 and Water Code §375 do not authorize charging above cost of service; the conservation mandate is achievable *within* cost-based tiering (a genuine marginal-cost rate already sends the conservation price signal), so 218's ceiling controls without a true constitutional conflict. [delphi, dialectic, tetlock, structural]
- The 30% recycled-water debt-service tranche is the most defensible allocation, plausibly traceable to the peaking/drought cost driver. [delphi, dialectic (antithesis/synthesis), steelman, tetlock]
- The 15% "water ambassador" staffing line is a distinct vulnerability as general-public-benefit spending (§6(b)(5)/(b)(2)), absent proof of targeting to surcharge payors. [delphi, dialectic, tetlock, steelman (anticipated as objection)]
- Rebates + ambassadors together put roughly 70% of the $4.1M revenue stream at risk on the use theory alone, without resolving the harder rate-math question. [delphi, dialectic]
- Attorney-fee exposure under CCP §1021.5 is a realistic and material component of exposure if plaintiffs prevail on any part. [dialectic, premortem, structural, tetlock, machinery]
- The ~9% protest result establishes procedural (§6(a)) compliance only and does not cure a substantive §6(b) defect (*Plantier*, unverified); it may, however, bear on remedy scope and fee-shifting equities. [dialectic, premortem, machinery, steelman]
- Converged repair path: recompute/cap the surcharge at a documented cost-derived ceiling and strip incentive-pricing language; move rebates (and possibly ambassador funding) off fee revenue to general fund/grants or a voter-approved special tax; verify/reallocate recycled-water debt service against actual demand-driver data. [dialectic, tetlock, structural, premortem, delphi]
- Bottom-line direction: a court more likely than not invalidates the surcharge at least in substantial part (delphi final median ~73%; tetlock estimates ~90%+ probability of at least a partial §6(b) violation and ~5–10% odds of a fully intact defense; dialectic synthesis concludes "more likely true than false"). Note the premortem assumes failure by design and the steelman is constructed contrarian, so neither is an independent vote. [delphi, tetlock, dialectic]
- Key deference authorities (*Coziahr*, the two *Griffith* cases, *Wilde*) are cited inconsistently across sources and must be treated as unverified rather than load-bearing. [delphi, dialectic, tetlock, steelman]

### Contested (lenses genuinely conflict)

- **Process vs. result reading of Capistrano.** One side: the "incentive pricing" admission is close to dispositive on the increment above cost regardless of arithmetic [delphi (majority), dialectic (thesis), premortem]. Other side: §6(b)(3) tests only the *result* — if $0.85 sits at or below the 1.6×-derived ceiling, the language is harmless surplusage/bad drafting, and *Capistrano* is distinguishable as a no-cost-study-at-all case [dialectic (antithesis), steelman, tetlock (conditional in Q2)]. *Nature: legal-question (what Capistrano requires), compounded by a missing fact (the dollar gap).*
- **The systemwide-benefit / DSM defense of the rebate tranche.** One side: demand-side management is a recognized cost of service; class-level, systemwide benefit suffices and dollar-for-dollar tracing has never been required [steelman, dialectic (antithesis), delphi (disagreement #5), tetlock (Q3 — no case makes rebate funding per se invalid; common utility practice)]. Other side: the district's own concession forecloses the theory on this record, and no quantified avoided-cost showing exists to carry the agency's burden [delphi (majority), dialectic (synthesis), premortem]. *Nature: factual-assumption/record-characterization layered on a genuinely unsettled legal question.*
- **Whether partial invalidation satisfies "substantial part."** One side: the 55% tranche alone (~$2.26M/yr) settles it [delphi (high panelist, 75/80 positions), dialectic (synthesis), tetlock]. Other side: meaningful probability remains of a narrow trim/severance/remand a fair observer would call a partial district win [delphi (72–73 cluster), dialectic (antithesis — modest trim limited to ambassador component)]. *Nature: interpretive framing of the proposition plus risk-tolerance.*
- **Prop 26 tax reclassification as independent exposure.** One side: a fee failing §6(b) falls outside the Art. XIII C §1(e) exemption and reverts to a tax requiring two-thirds voter approval — a colorable alternative theory and settlement lever [dialectic (thesis), machinery]. Other side: XIII D and XIII C are mutually exclusive tracks; a defective fee gets recalculation/refund under XIII D's own scheme, not retroactive tax status; largely foreclosed once fee characterization holds [dialectic (antithesis/synthesis), steelman, tetlock]. Both sides note no controlling authority. *Nature: legal-question, unresolved.*
- **The recycled-water 30%.** One side: likely the district's *strongest* component if plant sizing was driven by high-tier discretionary demand [dialectic (antithesis), delphi (Theme H), steelman, tetlock]. Other side: a discovery target *against* the district — if the plant is a systemwide reliability asset, loading 100% of its debt service on surcharge payers is itself a §6(b)(3) violation [dialectic (thesis)]. *Nature: factual-assumption (engineering/demand data).*
- **Directional inference from the Coziahr citation chaos.** Three positions within one lens: symmetric uncertainty (could cut either way), zero weight (a placeholder for "deference exists somewhere"), and asymmetric discount *against* the district's deference shield [delphi (disagreement #3)]; separately, one lens treats a confirmed 2024 *Coziahr* as potentially *strengthening* the district materially [steelman, tetlock]. *Nature: risk-tolerance/epistemic policy toward unverified authority.*
- **Mid-litigation restructuring/settlement.** One side: defensive restructuring toward the 1.6× figure is the sensible exposure-capping move, and bond pressure will drive the district there [premortem (assumed position), structural]. Other side: done without clean rescission and a fresh §6(a) notice-and-protest cycle, restructuring reads as an admission, spawns a new-fee procedural challenge, and can expand rather than cap the refund window [premortem (cause #8)]. *Nature: procedural/tactical, factual-assumption about execution.*
- **Refund look-back period.** One side suggests exposure could reach back multiple billing years (possible 3-year CCP §338(a) analysis, unverified) [dialectic (thesis), tetlock (Q5)]; another cites a 120-day limitations period under Gov. Code §53759 (unverified) that could sharply bound or even bar the claim [steelman, machinery]. *Nature: legal-question/authority-verification with large exposure consequences.*
- **What *Wilde* and which *Griffith* stand for.** Burden-of-proof holding vs. rate-deference holding for *Wilde*; *Griffith v. Pajaro Valley* vs. *Griffith v. City of Santa Cruz* (with two different pinpoint cites for the latter — 207 Cal.App.4th 1130 [delphi] vs. 1064 [tetlock]). [delphi (disagreements #3, #4), dialectic, tetlock] *Nature: authority-verification.*

- Associational-standing bifurcation: non-surcharged members may lack standing on the per-parcel proportionality claim while retaining it on a Prop 26 theory. [machinery]

- Discovery risk of a "budget-first, cost-story-second" paper trail — workpapers showing $0.85 was reverse-engineered from the $4.1M revenue target, the sequencing *Capistrano* criticized. [premortem]

## 2. Blind spots

- **Threshold procedure vs. merits.** Six of seven lenses litigated the merits on the implicit assumption the suit is timely and properly vehicled. Only [machinery] surfaced the 120-day Gov. Code §53759 clock, the 60-day reverse-validation question, and Claims Act presentation — any one of which could moot the entire merits analysis the other lenses produced. Conversely, [machinery] alone flags that the answer may differ *per theory* (short fuse for §6(b), longer clock for Prop 26).
- **The money behind the litigation.** Only [structural] and [premortem] engaged the bond covenants, ratings exposure, and settlement architecture; the doctrinal lenses (delphi, dialectic, tetlock, steelman) treat remedy as a judicial output rather than a negotiated one shaped by debt-service protection.
- **Discovery-generated risk.** Only [premortem] flagged the reverse-engineered-revenue-target scenario in consultant workpapers, and only [premortem] flagged that a defensive restructuring executed badly becomes evidence against the district.
- **Absent parties.** Only [structural] noted the rebate beneficiaries' total absence from the case and the distributive cost of any "clean" fix; only [machinery] noted bond-trustee joinder.
- **Under-explored across ALL lenses (noted absences, not analysis):** (1) every lens declares the $0.85-vs-1.6× dollar gap outcome-determinative, yet none attempts even a bounded or scenario-based estimate of it — the case's acknowledged center of gravity remains a blank across all seven outputs; (2) the stipulated fact that rebates "prioritize low-income customers" is engaged politically by [structural] and not examined by any lens as a distinct legal or programmatic consideration; (3) class-certification/refund-administration mechanics appear only in passing (a one-line assumption in [premortem]).

## 5. Completeness addendum (second pass — recovered from lens outputs after the draft dropped it)

- The governing standard of review is never stated in the draft: independent judicial review — not rational-basis deference — applies to §6(b) compliance, and the agency's own conclusion that its methodology is cost-based gets no deference (*Capistrano*; *Silicon Valley Taxpayers* by analogy), refined by the synthesis rule that agencies keep reasonable latitude on joint/common-cost allocation but none for substituting policy-effectiveness reasoning for cost causation. This shapes briefing posture on both sides. [dialectic, steelman]
- Remedy counterweight to the draft's severance-leaning convergent framing: courts are reluctant to judicially re-price rates (a legislative function) and tend to invalidate the rate as adopted and remand; because the rate-derivation and revenue-use defects overlap inside one blended $0.85 figure, "the excess" is analytically hard to isolate — tetlock puts ~58% on a broader-than-severance remedy given liability, and flags that severance administrability turns on whether the resolution/workpapers itemize a discrete cost-based sub-rate versus a single blended number (a checkable record fact). [tetlock]
- Plaintiff-side tactical item: deploy the rate study's incentive-pricing admission in a motion for summary adjudication on the §6(b)(3) claim, on the theory the admission negates the district's ability to carry its §6(b)(5) burden without trial. [dialectic]
- *Morgan v. Imperial Irrigation District* (unverified) should be promoted from footnote to the centerpiece of the defense — tiered rates upheld where the differential tracked actual procurement/peaking costs with no incentive markup; both thesis and antithesis independently name it the district's best precedent if the arithmetic holds. The draft lists Morgan only as a verification item. [dialectic, steelman]
- Pleading the "not a property-related fee" fallback is affirmatively counterproductive, not a costless hedge: it costs credibility with the court, hands plaintiffs a "district doesn't understand its own exposure" narrative, and pleading "not a fee" alongside "cost-justified fee" reads as weakness on the primary theory — the actionable advice is to consider dropping it entirely, stronger than the draft's "should not be its lead argument." [premortem, structural]
- Prejudgment interest on any refund award (Civ. Code §§3287/3289; Cal. Const. art. XV, §1 — confirm rate applicable against a public entity) is an exposure component absent from the draft's refund/fee accounting. [machinery]
- Exposure modeling must include the composite partial-loss-plus-§1021.5-award scenario, benchmarked against comparable Prop 218 fee awards (including any Capistrano remand award), before the board chooses litigate-vs-settle — the assumed failure mode was pricing only the excess-refund scenario. [premortem]
- Art. XIII D §6(c)'s exemption of water/sewer/refuse fees from the election requirement — the reason no ratifying election was ever held — should be confirmed, since it becomes central to the Prop 26 reclassification theory if §6(b) compliance fails. [machinery]
- Gov. Code §66022, cited by one delphi panelist, is a candidate alternate citation for the 120-day limitations period and should be run down alongside the §53759 verification the draft already flags. [delphi]
- Unpled §6(a) procedural checks beyond the notice-content item the draft recovered: confirm the ≥45-day mailing window (§6(a)(1)/Gov. Code §53755) against the actual hearing date, and audit the protest tabulation (denominator used — parcels vs. other unit — and who was counted). [machinery]
- Defense-side threshold checks: verify (not assume) that a plaintiff need not have personally lodged a protest at the hearing to preserve standing to sue, and whether the district's own code contains an internal rate-dispute/appeal procedure supporting an exhaustion defense. [machinery]
- Cure-design details missing from the draft's cure bullet: whether any interim rate is available or the district must revert to the pre-surcharge rate during re-adoption; whether the original §6(a) notice reserved authority to reallocate restricted-fund spending without a fresh cycle; and whether the board can issue voluntary refunds/credits without a court order — and from what fund, given §6(b)(2). [machinery, tetlock]
- §1021.5 incentives constrain settlement architecture: fee-shifting pushes plaintiffs' counsel toward maximal, published, precedent-setting relief rather than a quiet rate tweak, and any settlement offer is shaped by what preserves a colorable fee petition — a driver of settlement shape independent of the members' economic grievance. [structural]
- Check for State Water Board/DWR guidance or model-rate materials on Prop 218-compliant conservation-tier pricing (those agencies have an institutional incentive to have produced it) as potential support for the district's methodology; also check whether an environmental-justice/legal-aid organization could appear as amicus for the otherwise-unrepresented rebate beneficiaries. [structural]
- Repair option omitted from the converged blueprint: migrate toward individualized water-budget-based tiers tied to parcel-specific characteristics (lot size, occupancy), per *City of Palmdale* (unverified), to strengthen the parcel-attributable-cost nexus. [dialectic]
