## 1. Argument map

### Convergent (multiple independent lenses agree)

- The surcharge is almost certainly a "property-related fee" subject to art. XIII D, § 6(b); the district's "not a fee" fallback is weak-to-foreclosed under *Bighorn* (unverified), forcing the case onto the § 6(b) merits. [delphi, dialectic, premortem, structural, tetlock] (steelman constructs the contrary argument but itself concedes it is "the weakest branch" of the defense) [steelman]
- The rate study's own "derived in part from conservation-incentive pricing" language is the district's central vulnerability, tracking the defect condemned in *Capistrano* (unverified) and functioning as a near-admission on the record. [delphi, dialectic, premortem, tetlock]
- The 55%-rebate allocation to conceded non-payers is a second, analytically independent theory of invalidation under §§ 6(b)(1)–(3) — a disjunctive structure meaning the district must win both theories while plaintiffs need only one. [delphi, dialectic, premortem, tetlock]
- The documented 1.6× cost differential is a genuine cost anchor that makes severance/partial invalidation more likely than wholesale invalidation, and distinguishes this record from *Capistrano*'s. [delphi, dialectic, steelman, tetlock]
- § 6(b)(5)'s burden of proof on the agency, combined with its own record concessions, puts the district in an unusually bad litigation posture. [delphi, dialectic, premortem, tetlock]
- Art. X, § 2 / Water Code § 375 do not override Prop 218; the dominant reconciliation is that cost-based tiering already delivers the conservation price signal, and any above-cost increment requires other funding or voter approval. [delphi, dialectic, structural, tetlock]
- The unquantified dollar gap between $0.85 and what a strict 1.6× derivation would yield is the single most important unresolved fact in the case. [delphi, dialectic, steelman, tetlock]
- Directional bottom line among lenses that reached one: invalidation in substantial part is more likely than not (delphi final median 73; dialectic synthesis "more likely true than false"; tetlock ≥90% for at least partial violation, ~5–10% full district defense). [delphi, dialectic, tetlock]
- Attorney-fee exposure under CCP § 1021.5 (unverified) is material and often undercounted, on top of refund liability under the Gov. Code § 53750 et seq. scheme (unverified). [dialectic, premortem, structural, tetlock]
- Repair path convergence: cap the rate at a documented cost-derived ceiling, strip the incentive-pricing language, move the rebate (and possibly education) spending off fee revenue to general fund/grants/a voter-approved special tax. [dialectic, premortem, structural, tetlock]
- The 30% recycled-water debt-service tranche is the most defensible piece of the fund allocation, contingent on engineering data showing peak-driven plant sizing. [delphi, dialectic, steelman, tetlock]
- Highest-priority evidence: the rate-study workpapers and the base Tier 2 rate, to do the arithmetic everyone agrees is the right test. [delphi, dialectic, premortem, steelman, tetlock]

### Contested (lenses genuinely conflict)

- **Is the incentive-pricing admission near-dispositive, or curable surplusage?** Near-dispositive: an admission against interest that does plaintiffs' work under the agency's burden [delphi (majority), dialectic (thesis; synthesis "as the record stands"), premortem]. Curable: § 6(b)(3) is a *result* test measured in dollars, not adjectives — if $0.85 sits at/below the 1.6× ceiling the label is harmless rhetoric [dialectic (antithesis), steelman, tetlock (Q2 counterfactual), delphi (deference-theme minority)]. *Nature: legal question (process- vs. result-reading of Capistrano) compounded by a missing fact (the gap).*
- **Viability of the systemwide-benefit / demand-side-management defense of the rebates.** Foreclosed on this record by the district's own concession [delphi (75/80 panelists), dialectic (synthesis), premortem]. Live and colorable — no case squarely holds rebate-funded rates per se invalid; DSM is common utility practice; class-level benefit may suffice [delphi (72–73 cluster), dialectic (antithesis), steelman, tetlock (Q3)]. *Nature: record-characterization (factual) layered on an unsettled legal question.*
- **The 15% "water ambassador" tranche.** A distinct general-public-benefit vulnerability under § 6(b)(5), expanding exposure to ~70% of revenue [delphi, dialectic]. Comparatively defensible demand-management spending, more tolerable than the rebates [steelman, tetlock (Q3/Q6)]. *Nature: legal question plus a factual one (how staff time is actually allocated).*
- **Prop 26 tax-conversion theory.** A noncompliant § 6(b) fee falls outside the art. XIII C, § 1(e) exemption and reverts to a tax requiring a two-thirds vote — real alternative exposure [dialectic (thesis)]. The two articles are mutually exclusive tracks; a defective fee stays a defective fee with Art. XIII D remedies; plead in the alternative only [dialectic (antithesis + synthesis), steelman, tetlock]. *Nature: legal question — no lens identified controlling authority either way.*
- **Whether partial invalidation satisfies "in substantial part."** The 55% tranche alone settles it [delphi (high panelist), dialectic (synthesis)]. Meaningful probability remains of a narrow trim/remand that a fair observer would call a district win [delphi (72–73 cluster), dialectic (antithesis)]. *Nature: interpretive/risk-tolerance about the proposition's framing plus remedy prediction.*
- **Remedy scope.** Severance/capping of discrete tranches and increments [delphi, dialectic, steelman]. Whole-rate taint — courts are reluctant to reprice, and an integrated $0.85 figure may not be decomposable [premortem, tetlock (Q5 leans toward broader remedy)]. *Nature: legal remedy prediction.*
- **Whether pleading the "not a fee" fallback is a costless hedge or affirmatively harmful.** Preserve it — non-frivolous, with textual and *Griffith*-analogy support [steelman]. It is dead on arrival and costs credibility on everything else / signals weakness on the primary theory [premortem, structural]. *Nature: risk-tolerance/litigation-strategy judgment.*
- **Mid-litigation restructuring.** District can amend prospectively to cap exposure, possibly without full reprocessing [structural, tetlock (Q6, uncertain under Gov. Code § 53750(h)(1), unverified)]. Restructuring backfired when done without clean rescission and a fresh § 6(a) cycle, and was used as an admission [premortem]. *Nature: legal question plus execution-risk assumption.*
- **How to weight the unverified *Coziahr* authority.** Delphi split three ways (symmetric uncertainty / zero weight / asymmetric discount against the district's deference shield) [delphi]; steelman treats it, if confirmed, as potentially materially strengthening the district [steelman]; tetlock treats it as the single research item most likely to move the estimate either way [tetlock]. *Nature: epistemic method / risk-tolerance toward unverified authority.*
- **Applicable refund look-back regime — the lenses propose incompatible unverified candidates:** CCP § 338(a) three-year vs. reverse-validation (CCP § 860 et seq. / Gov. Code § 53511) [dialectic]; Gov. Code § 53759 120-day period [steelman]; Gov. Code claims-presentation, historically ~1 year [structural]; Gov. Code §§ 900/935 plus CCP § 338 [tetlock]. *Nature: legal question, entirely unverified across all lenses.*
- **The recycled-water plant (30%).** Likely survives as textbook cost-causation if sized for discretionary high-tier demand [dialectic (antithesis/synthesis), delphi, steelman, tetlock] vs. a possible additional vulnerability if it is a systemwide asset improperly loaded 100% onto surcharge payers [dialectic (thesis)]. *Nature: factual (plant engineering/sizing).*

## 2. Blind spots

- **Bond covenant / public-finance fallout** was caught only by two lenses: the 30% debt-service pledge means an adverse ruling is a ratings/covenant event, and bondholder pressure — not doctrine — likely sets the district's settlement floor. The doctrinal lenses (delphi, dialectic, steelman, tetlock) walked past it. [premortem, structural]
- **The cure/restructuring trap** — that a mid-litigation rate fix has its own § 6(a)/Gov. Code procedural prerequisites and can function as an admission expanding the refund window — appears only in premortem (and glancingly in tetlock Q6). [premortem, tetlock]
- **Workpaper-sequencing discovery risk** (revenue target set before cost analysis) is premortem-only, despite every lens treating the workpapers as the key evidence. [premortem]
- **The behavioral aftermath of any ruling** (consultants relabeling, cosmetic compliance, the rebate program becoming budget-vulnerable once decoupled) is structural-only. [structural]
- **Prop 26's § 1(e)(2) specific-service exception** as an affirmative fallback appears only in steelman and a tetlock crux note. [steelman, tetlock]
- Under-explored across ALL lenses (absences noted, no analysis added): no lens examined the owner-vs.-tenant/customer dimension of "incident of property ownership" for a usage-triggered surcharge; no lens developed the procedural vehicle question (validation action vs. ordinary civil action, and record-scope limits) beyond a single dialectic flag; and refund-administration/class mechanics appear only as one passing line in premortem's retrospective.

## 4. Noise audit

- **delphi** is the heaviest source of duplication: seven panelists restate the same three-pillar structure, and the final-round bullets largely re-narrate the facilitator summary back at itself; a good portion of the text is panel-process meta-commentary ("I did not move toward the 78 outlier") rather than analysis. Its genuine signal is concentrated in the 72–80 spread, the four persistent-disagreement items, and the citation-instability finding — perhaps a quarter of its length.
- **tetlock** self-flags that Q4 double-counts Q2, which is honest but means one of six cruxes is redundant; Q6's "P=60" is not a coherent probability of anything (the crux is a how-to question, not a proposition); and the recomposition arithmetic (0.85 × 0.15 × 0.30 ≈ 3.8%, with an ad hoc "call it 30%" adjustment) dresses judgment in decorated precision. The tree structure and decisive-crux identification are real contributions.
- **dialectic** is substantive but internally repetitive: the repair blueprint appears twice (thesis § X, synthesis § 11), exposure figures three times, and much of the synthesis re-derives points already stated by thesis and antithesis. The antithesis's process-vs.-result move and the synthesis's axis decomposition are its distinctive value.
- **structural** repeats its bond-pressure and consultant-adaptation points across three separate sections; moderate padding by restatement, but its unique content (absent stakeholders, candor penalty, class turnover) is not duplicated anywhere else in the panel.
- **premortem** and **steelman** are comparatively tight and on-task; no material padding identified in either.
