## What revision actually added (the honest list)

**1. But note: the *argument* existed in R1ONLY; revision added weighting and arithmetic, not a new theory.

**2. The overdetermination thesis.** FULL's high panelist crystallized something R1ONLY only gestures at: the 55% rebate tranche of a $4.1M fund, with the district's own concession, plausibly satisfies "substantial part" *on its own*, making the unquantified pricing gap irrelevant to the outcome. R1ONLY treats the pricing gap as "the crux"; FULL surfaces a live split about whether the gap even matters. For district-side counsel this changes advice ordering: fix the revenue-use allocations first — re-deriving the $0.85 rate is necessary but not sufficient. This is the single best output of the revision rounds. It's also foreshadowed in R1ONLY (Panelist 6: severing the rebate-funded share alone renders the proposition true; multiple panelists calling the cross-subsidy "self-proving"), so it's a sharpening, not a discovery.

A lawyer reading only R1ONLY plus its facilitator report gets ~90% of the actionable content: same bottom-line probability band, same three-pillar analysis, same defense triage, same verification list.
