- **Gov. ## Three items whose deadlines/preconditions are most easily missed

1. **The "action taken" precondition (Gov. Code §54952.6) for the §54960.1 cure-and-correct demand.** If no Harbor Yards vote has actually occurred, the demand may be aimed at a remedy (nullify a past action) that has no target yet — while the real fit may be §54960's broader, precondition-free injunctive/declaratory action. Treating §54960.1's cure clock as *the* governing clock when it may not even apply is the single biggest structural trap here.

2. **The §54952.2(c) safe-harbor's own anti-relay proviso, independent of the "is AI an intermediary" question.** Attention will gravitate to whether software can be an "intermediary" or whether it fits the staff-briefing exception at all — but if the exception's text conditions safe-harbor status on *not* relaying one member's position to another, CivicAssist's cross-attribution may defeat the exception on its own terms regardless of how the AI-personhood question comes out. Confirm the exact text before either analysis proceeds.

3. **The litigation-hold/records-retention precondition (Gov. Code §34090 + common-law duty) running now across all five accounts, not just the four known users.** Because shared memory means the fifth member's account may hold ingested content too, and because the vendor's own memory/retention settings could auto-purge data on a schedule nobody has checked, this preservation duty is live today and easy to under-scope by focusing only on "the four members who used it."
