## 1. Combination logic — three tracks, three different operators

This is **not** one question with six inputs; it's three legally distinct exposures (statutory/SLA, constitutional/gift-clause, civil-rights/disparate-impact) that combine **disjunctively** at the top level — the City loses something if Track A **or** Track B **or** Track C lands — but each track has its own internal logic, and the tracks are not all independent of each other.

**Track A — SLA/HCD (Q1 → Q2 → Q3): a conjunctive chain, sequential/conditional.**
Q2 only matters if Q1 goes against the City (no exemption); Q3's penalty question only matters if Q2 also goes against the City (no cure). This is `P(no exemption) × P(not cured)` to get "live, uncured violation," and then Q3 is a *severity* multiplier on top, not an independent liability gate.

**Track B — Gift clause (Q4): nominally freestanding, but conditionally coupled to Track A.**
Doctrinally, Janssen-line deference means Track B doesn't legally require an SLA violation to succeed or fail. But evidentially, Q4's own analysis flags the SLA violation as the taxpayer plaintiffs' best available proof that the council's "public purpose" recitation was pretextual or record-deficient. So Track B isn't a clean independent draw — its probability should be read as **conditioned upward** by however Track A resolves. This is the one place where "disjunctive across tracks" breaks down into a soft dependency.

**Track C — Disparate impact (Q5): genuinely orthogonal.**
Q6's own analysis is explicit that nothing in the exemption fight or the gift-clause defense touches the *Inclusive Communities* three-step framework. Track C lives and dies on demographic/applicant-pool proof and the preference's design — independent of A and B in both directions.

**Q6 is the remedy-recomposition layer, not a fourth track.** It shows the *fixes* don't map 1:1 onto the *claims*: raising the affordable percentage/covenant term is a fix that helps A and B simultaneously (synergistic); redesigning the preference is a fix that helps only C (severable, does nothing for A/B); and re-noticing/re-running priority negotiations under §54222 — the one fix Track A may actually *require* — is in tension with deal finality (Pacifica's contract/reliance interests), which none of the other fixes touch. So the combination logic for the remedy side is: **two levers converge, one lever is severable, and one lever may be irreducible without unwind.**
