### Persistent disagreement (signal, not noise)

# Persistent Disagreement Report — Final Round

The panel converged tightly (range 80–84, σ 1.2), but four distinct disagreements survived mutual feedback. Panelists explicitly engaged each other's points and *declined to move* — so these are considered splits, not oversights.

---

## 1. What "substantially prevail" means (the largest persistent split)

**Crux, lower cluster (80, 81, 81):** The proposition's "held unlawful / substantially prevail" framing imports remedy-stage outcomes. If a court agrees with HCD's characterization but narrows relief to penalties-plus-prospective-compliance, credits a cure period, or blesses a negotiated resolution short of unwinding the DDA, the proposition might not resolve as "correct." One panelist stated this "remains a legitimate basis" for staying lower; another called it "a genuine ceiling on confidence rather than resolving it either way."

**Crux, upper cluster (82, 82, 82, 84):** The proposition's own parenthetical confines the question to exemption-qualification and process-compliance — a merits characterization. Remedy-softening doctrines (substantial compliance, estoppel, laches, cure) "go to scope of relief rather than the legal-characterization question the proposition actually asks" and are therefore orthogonal.

**Source of split:** Primarily a *proposition-interpretation* disagreement (how to read "substantially prevail"), layered over a *genuinely unsettled legal question* — how much daylight California courts allow between finding an SLA violation and ordering full invalidation.

**What would resolve it:** A precise operationalization of the proposition's resolution criteria; or, on the legal layer, authority establishing whether SLA violations post-AB 1486 support DDA invalidation versus §54230.5 penalties with prospective compliance, and whether substantial-compliance/cure defenses are cognizable at all.

---

## 2. NOA violation: freestanding second pillar vs. derivative consequence

**Crux, majority view (roughly 5 of 7):** The §54222 Notice-of-Availability failure is an *independently sufficient* violation — mechanically provable, undisputed on the facts — giving HCD a second path to prevail even if the exemption analysis proves closer than expected. One panelist emphasized the facts show "a total failure to notice rather than a documented but erroneous exemption finding."

**Crux, minority view (2 panelists, one of whom adopted this framing during the final round):** The NOA duty attaches only to non-exempt surplus land, so the notice failure is "the automatic consequence of the exemption claim failing, not a second freestanding violation." The case rests on "one strong, unified pillar," which one panelist expressly cited as a reason to cap confidence in the low-80s rather than push toward 85–90.

**Source of split:** A disagreement about *legal architecture* — statutory structure of §54221(f)/§54222 — not about outcome direction. All seven agree the process was owed and skipped. But it functions as a *risk-tolerance* driver: those who see two independent grounds price in more redundancy against error.

**What would resolve it:** A court's characterization of whether an NOA violation can be found and remedied independently of resolving the exemption question, or facts showing whether the city made any documented exemption finding before entering the ENA.

---

## 3. How much weight convergence-despite-divergent-recollections deserves

**Crux, upper end (84, and the movers from 80→82):** The fact that seven panelists reconstructed *incompatible* statutory thresholds yet all found the deal fails is "cross-validating evidence" — a robustness signal warranting upward movement.

**Crux, lower end (80):** That same convergence is discounted because "convergence among people relying on the same imperfect memory is weaker evidence than convergence among people independently verifying primary text" — a shared limitation cannot substitute for verification, which "caps how far I am willing to raise my estimate on that basis alone."

**Source of split:** Pure *epistemic risk tolerance* applied to a shared, acknowledged factual gap (all 7 self-flagged reliance on memory across the AB 1486 / AB 1255 / SB 6 / AB 2135 / AB 480 amendment cycles). No panelist disputes the gap exists; they disagree on how much the convergence pattern offsets it.

**What would resolve it:** The verified current text of Gov. Code §54221(f) — exact lower-income percentage, covenant-duration floor, and subsection location — including whether any later amendment created a mixed-income, transit-oriented, cure, or safe-harbor pathway that could accommodate a 30%-affordable / 25-year structure. Multiple panelists identified this as the single fact most capable of changing the analysis.

---

## 4. Minor residual variables (raised, not resolved)

- **Charter-city status:** One panelist originally raised the §54234 / statewide-concern point; two credited it as removing a defense and moved up partly on it; the panelist at 84 noted Vista Camino's charter status is *unstated in the facts* and kept it on the uncertainty list. Resolvable by a single fact (is the city charter or general-law) plus verification of the SLA's charter-city coverage.
- **Unrecalled alternative exemption:** All panelists note no plausible alternative §54221(f) category was identified, but the lower cluster preserves residual probability for an "obscure alternate exemption category" or grandfathering provision none of them recalled; the upper cluster treats the exhaustive-search failure as affirmative evidence. Resolvable only against the full current statutory text.

---

## Summary characterization

The panel is unanimous on the qualitative conclusion (deal fails the exemption under every recalled threshold variant; notice process was skipped). The persistent 4-point spread traces to: **(1)** an interpretive question about whether the proposition encompasses remedy outcomes — partly definitional, partly a genuinely unsettled remedies question under the SLA; **(2)** a structural disagreement about whether HCD has one ground or two; and **(3)** differing tolerance for the shared, unverified-statutory-text gap. None of the residual disagreement is about the facts of the disposition itself; all of it would collapse or sharpen upon (i) the verified current text of §54221(f)–§54222 and §54230.5, (ii) authority on SLA remedy scope, and (iii) two case-specific facts (charter status; whether the city documented an exemption finding before the ENA).
