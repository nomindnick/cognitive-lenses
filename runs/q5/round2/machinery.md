## I. SURPLUS LAND ACT — HCD Administrative Track

- **Exempt-surplus-land declaration threshold.** Gov. Code § 54221(f) (exempt categories) — trigger: agency's classification of parcel before disposition — verify exact income-mix/percentage and durational thresholds in the specific exempt-category subdivision the city relied on, and whether an 18-low/18-moderate split satisfies a "lower income" percentage test or whether moderate-income units fail to count.
- **Covenant-duration requirement.** Gov. Code § 54221(f) exempt-housing category and/or § 54230.5 general disposition terms — verify whether the applicable minimum recorded-covenant term is 55 years (rental)/45 years (ownership) and whether the 25-year term is independently disqualifying regardless of exemption status.
- **Notice-of-availability duty.** Gov. Code § 54222 — trigger: intent to dispose of non-exempt surplus land — verify NOA is required to precede execution of the ENA (not just the DDA), and confirm the full list of statutorily entitled recipient entities (housing sponsors, school districts, park/rec agencies, etc.) the city allegedly bypassed.
- **60-day interest-notification window.** Gov. Code § 54222/§54224 (verify exact number) — trigger: NOA mailing date — verify precise day count for recipient entities to notify interest.
- **Good-faith negotiation period.** Gov. Code § 54224 — trigger: entity notifies interest — verify minimum negotiation period (recalled ~90 days) before agency may negotiate with any other party.
- **Priority for higher-affordability responder.** Gov. Code § 54222.5 — verify current percentage threshold (25%+) that triggers negotiating priority among competing housing-sponsor respondents.
- **HCD notice/self-certification of exemption.** Gov. Code § 54221(f)(2) or § 54230(b) (verify) — trigger: agency's exemption determination — verify whether written notice to HCD of the claimed exemption is a mandatory prerequisite (not merely advisory) and whether the city's unilateral declaration without HCD sign-off is itself a separate defect.
- **Annual surplus-land inventory/reporting.** Gov. Code § 54230(e) / Housing Element Annual Progress Report cross-reference (Gov. Code § 65400) — verify whether this parcel was required to be disclosed in APR filings and whether omission bears on HCD's NOV.
- **Notice of Violation → response/cure sequence.** Likely governed by HCD's SLA regulations, not the Gov. Code itself — verify citation (believed 25 Cal. Code Regs. §§ 6300 et seq.) and confirm the agency's response deadline, any cure opportunity, and when the penalty clock actually starts relative to NOV issuance vs. HCD's final determination.
- **Penalty amount and escalation.** Gov. Code § 54234 (verify section number) — trigger: HCD final determination of noncompliance — verify 30% (first violation) / 50% (subsequent violation within 5 years) figures and whether "disposition price or fair market value, whichever greater" is the correct formula.
- **Penalty proceeds destination (offramp).** Gov. Code § 54234 (verify subsection) — verify whether penalty funds must be deposited into a local low/moderate-income housing fund or the state Building Homes and Jobs Trust Fund, and whether directed reinvestment functions as a statutory mitigation/offramp.
- **HCD's remedial scope.** Verify whether HCD itself can order rescission/unwinding of a closed conveyance or whether its authority is limited to penalty assessment plus referral for injunctive relief (i.e., confirm recordation status of the grant deed and its effect on available remedies).
- **AG enforcement referral.** Gov. Code § 54230.5/§54234 (verify) — trigger: HCD referral — verify AG's authority to seek a compliance order and whether AG action is a precondition to any court-ordered rescission.
- **Density Bonus Law cross-check.** Gov. Code § 65915(c) — verify whether the 120-unit project claimed density-bonus concessions, which independently impose a 55-year affordability covenant minimum regardless of SLA exemption status.
- **State-funded program nondiscrimination overlay.** Gov. Code § 11135 and implementing regs (2 CCR § 11154 et seq., verify) — verify whether the project receives state subsidy (LIHTC, MHP, AHSC, SB 2, NPLH) beyond the land transfer, which would independently constrain the community-preference covenant.

## II. SURPLUS LAND ACT — Judicial/Third-Party Vehicles

- **Private right of action.** Gov. Code § 54230.5/§54234 (verify subsection) — verify whether "any interested party" may sue to compel NOA compliance, and whether this reaches penalty recovery or only injunctive/declaratory relief.
- **Standing for third-party SLA suit.** Verify statutory standing language ("interested party") against general taxpayer/citizen standing doctrines; confirm whether the taxpayer association or fair-housing org independently qualifies.
- **Statute of limitations for SLA challenge.** No SLA-specific SOL clearly identified — verify whether courts borrow CCP § 338(a) (3-year, liability created by statute) or a shorter land-use-decision period; confirm accrual date (DDA execution vs. deed recordation vs. HCD's NOV).
- **Traditional mandamus (CCP § 1085).** Vehicle to compel city's ministerial NOA duty — verify ripeness given DDA already executed/possibly closed.
- **Administrative mandamus (CCP § 1094.5).** Vehicle to review HCD's final penalty determination if characterized as quasi-adjudicative — verify whether HCD's process meets the adjudicative-hearing predicate for § 1094.5 versus ordinary § 1085 review.
- **Validation action applicability.** CCP §§ 860–870 — verify whether any statute makes this specific DDA a "validatable" instrument (unclear absent former redevelopment-law hook); if not applicable, confirm ordinary declaratory relief (CCP § 1060) is the operative vehicle, with no 60-day validation SOL.
- **Successor Agency/LRPMP cross-check.** Health & Safety Code § 34191.5 — verify chain of title: if the parcel was ever redevelopment-agency property, Department of Finance approval of a Long-Range Property Management Plan may be an unaddressed prerequisite.
- **General municipal property-disposition statutes.** Gov. Code §§ 37420–37421 (or charter equivalent) — verify whether independent public-auction/competitive-bidding requirements for city real property apply outside the SLA framework, and whether any below-market-sale-for-housing exception (verify citation) was invoked/satisfied.

## III. GIFT OF PUBLIC FUNDS — Taxpayer Track (State Court)

- **Taxpayer standing.** Code Civ. Proc. § 526a — verify plaintiff association (or its members) qualifies as having paid, within one year before filing, a tax that funds the city, per current statutory language.
- **Alternative public-interest standing.** Verify applicability of common-law public-interest standing (cf. Waste Mgmt. of Alameda Cnty. v. Cnty. of Alameda) if § 526a requirements are not clearly met by an association plaintiff.
- **Governing limitations period.** Not fixed by art. XVI, § 6 itself — verify whether courts apply CCP § 343 (4-year residual), CCP § 337 (4-year, written contract), a shorter land-use-decision period (Gov. Code § 65009, verify 90-day trigger for "decisions" under planning/zoning law), or an accrual tied to deed recordation.
- **Relief available.** Verify whether art. XVI § 6 claims can support rescission of an already-recorded conveyance versus only prospective injunctive relief against further disbursement.
- **Fee-shifting offramp for prevailing plaintiff.** CCP § 1021.5 (private attorney general) — verify "significant benefit," "necessity," and financial-burden elements are independently satisfiable on these facts (procedural checklist only; no merits view expressed).
- **No statutory cure mechanism identified for the gift-clause claim itself** — verify whether any curative resolution (e.g., council re-adoption with revised findings) is legally capable of mooting a filed or threatened claim, versus requiring a formal settlement/consent judgment.

- **Organizational standing.** Havens Realty Corp. v. Coleman (verify continued application) — verify the fair-housing org can show concrete diversion-of-resources/frustration-of-mission injury, not merely abstract interest.
- **Disparate-impact regulatory framework.** 24 C.F.R. § 100.500 — verify current effective version (2013 rule, 2020 amendment, 2023 reinstatement — confirm which version governs burden-shifting elements as of filing date).
- **DOJ pattern-or-practice authority.** 42 U.S.C. § 3614 — trigger: HUD referral or independent DOJ investigation — verify preconditions for DOJ suit versus private/HUD track.
- **AFFH — federal.** 42 U.S.C. § 3608(e)(5) and HUD's AFFH rule — verify current regulatory status (rule has been through rescission/reinstatement cycles); confirm whether the city receives HUD funding (CDBG/HOME) that would trigger AFFH certification obligations independent of the FHA disparate-impact claim.
- **Fee-shifting.** 42 U.S.C. § 3613(c)(2) — prevailing-party attorney's fees; verify standard applied to prevailing defendants (typically frivolousness/bad-faith standard, asymmetric from prevailing plaintiffs).

## VII. Penalties, Fees, and Costs — Cross-Track Summary

- **SLA penalty:** runs against the "local agency" (verify whether it can reach the developer or only the city) — 30%/50% of disposition price or FMV — payable to housing fund (verify).
- **Gift-clause suit:** no statutory penalty; potential CCP § 1021.5 fee award to prevailing taxpayer plaintiff; potential rescission/restitution as equitable remedy (verify availability post-closing).
- **FHA suit:** prevailing-party fees under 42 U.S.C. § 3613(c)(2); no statutory penalty cap identified — verify availability of compensatory/punitive damages against a municipal defendant (note Eleventh Amendment/municipal immunity doctrines may limit punitive damages against the city — flag for merits component, not resolved here).

## Three items most likely to be missed

1. **The HCD NOV response/cure deadline**, which almost certainly lives in HCD's Surplus Land Act *regulations* (verify citation, believed 25 Cal. Code Regs. §§ 6300 et seq.) rather than in the Government Code sections practitioners typically pull up — missing this window converts a curable compliance issue into a locked-in 30% penalty determination.
2. **The CEQA 35-day Notice-of-Exemption challenge window** (Pub. Res. Code § 21167(f)) — a short, hard clock that runs independently of and much faster than the SLA/gift-clause/fair-housing tracks, and is easy to overlook because CEQA isn't one of the three headline exposures.
3. **The Government Claims Act presentation deadline** (Gov. Code §§ 911.2, 945.4) for any damages component of the fair-housing or gift-clause claims — a 6-month/1-year absolute bar that is frequently missed by plaintiffs focused on the federal/state administrative fair-housing timelines and by taxpayer plaintiffs who mistakenly assume § 526a injunctive actions carry no claim-presentation predicate for any damages aspect of their theory.
