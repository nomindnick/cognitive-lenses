## 1. Argument map

### Convergent (multiple independent lenses agree)

- **The exemption claim fails on the merits under every version of the (unverified) thresholds anyone recalled**: only 30% of units are restricted at all, only 15% (18/120) at a strictly "lower income" level, and the 25-year covenant falls well short of the ~45–55-year durations the lenses believe the affordable-housing exemption requires. [delphi, dialectic, steelman, tetlock, premortem]
- **No alternative § 54221(f) exemption category plausibly covers a $1 fee conveyance of a 4.2-acre former corporation yard to a private for-profit developer** (public-to-public, small-parcel, open-space, infeasibility, grandfathering all facially inapposite). [delphi, dialectic, steelman, tetlock]
- **The § 54222 Notice-of-Availability / priority-negotiation process was owed and entirely skipped** — a total failure to notice, not a defective notice; entering the ENA first foreclosed the competition the statute protects. [delphi, dialectic, steelman, tetlock, premortem]
- **HCD's position on liability very likely substantially prevails** — delphi's panel converged at 80–84%; tetlock's decomposition puts "no valid exemption" at ~92% and a live uncured violation at ~78%; dialectic's synthesis reaches the same bottom line reading the proposition as a liability question. [delphi, dialectic, tetlock]
- **The 30% penalty would be computed against the $11.8M FMV, not the $1 price** (greater-of formulation, unverified), yielding roughly $3.5M exposure. [delphi, dialectic, premortem, steelman, tetlock]
- **Liability and remedy are different questions**: a violation finding does not automatically unwind the DDA; the Act's express rescission authority is uncertain, and courts/institutions are reluctant to void completed conveyances. [delphi, dialectic, structural, tetlock]
- **Every SLA-merits conclusion rests on remembered, unverified statutory text** across the AB 1486 / AB 1255 / SB 6 / AB 2135 / AB 480 amendment cycles; verifying current § 54221(f) is the single highest-value step. [delphi, dialectic, tetlock]
- **The three exposures are coupled, not severable**: an SLA violation (or admissions made curing it) feeds the gift-clause pretext/consistency argument; deepening affordability helps both SLA and gift-clause positions; the preference fix is largely orthogonal. [dialectic, premortem, steelman, structural, tetlock]
- **The local preference carries real disparate-impact exposure under the *Inclusive Communities* framework given the 78%-vs-52% demographic skew, with facially available less-discriminatory alternatives** (displacement-based/right-to-return, non-residency criteria) — and an AFFH overlay (Gov. Code § 8899.50 / AB 686, unverified) that pressures redesign regardless of litigation outcome. [steelman, tetlock, premortem, structural]
- **Which exemption category the city's resolution actually invoked is an inference from the NOV, not a confirmed fact** — pull the resolution. [dialectic, tetlock, structural, delphi]
- **The ENA-before-any-notice sequencing functions as predetermination evidence** (and possibly a reverse-engineered exemption finding), discoverable from the ENA date vs. exemption-findings date. [dialectic, structural]
- **A charter-city / home-rule defense is flagged as weak but unverified**, and the city's charter status is unstated in the facts. [delphi, dialectic]

### Contested (lenses genuinely conflict)

- **NOA violation: freestanding second ground vs. derivative of the exemption call.** Delphi split 5–2 in favor of "independently sufficient"; dialectic's antithesis calls it "parasitic" (one legal determination, two labeled consequences), and dialectic's synthesis relabels it "doctrinally derivative but evidentially independent." [delphi, dialectic] — *Nature: legal question about statutory architecture; all sources agree on the bottom-line outcome.*
- **Scope of "substantially prevail": merits-only vs. remedy-inclusive.** Delphi's lower cluster (80–81) held remedy uncertainty (cure, substantial compliance, negotiated resolution) as a genuine ceiling; delphi's upper cluster and dialectic's synthesis read the proposition's parenthetical as confining it to the merits, making remedy orthogonal. [delphi, dialectic] — *Nature: proposition-interpretation, layered over a genuinely unsettled remedies question.*
- **Is cure-in-place via DDA amendment viable?** The conventional posture (amend covenants/mix, negotiate with HCD) is treated as structurally the most likely outcome [structural, dialectic]; but the steelman holds only full unwind-and-restart is compliant (retroactive notice "ratifies" the violation), the premortem posits HCD reading the NOV as "unlawful ab initio" and treating a slow workout as continued noncompliance, and tetlock estimates only ~15% that cure/functional compliance would be accepted. [structural, dialectic, steelman, premortem, tetlock] — *Nature: part legal (cure doctrine under the Act, unverified), part factual assumption about HCD's actual posture.*
- **Can the preference be saved by redesign?** Tetlock (~55% claim viability; "redesign is the fix"), structural (negotiated redesign the likely resolution), and the premortem's assumed position all treat redesign as available; the steelman argues dilution is "the same mechanism at smaller scale" and only elimination or de-linking from local tenure works; the premortem separately warns a redesign done for optics without a displacement/need record escalates exposure toward intentional-discrimination claims. [tetlock, structural, premortem, steelman] — *Nature: legal question (ICP steps two/three) plus factual assumption (displacement nexus, applicant-pool data).*
- **Agency deference.** Delphi (several panelists) and dialectic's thesis invoke judicial deference to HCD's interpretation (*Yamaha*, unverified); dialectic's antithesis argues *Yamaha*'s own factors cut against deference to a litigating-position NOV; dialectic's synthesis calls deference immaterial to the numeric core either way. [delphi, dialectic] — *Nature: legal question; possibly moot per the synthesis.*
- **Will an excluded housing sponsor actually enforce?** The premortem's top-ranked failure mode is a sponsor suit to void the deal (NOV publicity alerting exactly that audience, with possible independent enforcement rights HCD cannot waive); structural concludes the intended beneficiary class is "structurally the least likely enforcer" — invisible, uninformed, litigating over a hypothetical bid. Dialectic's antithesis also notes no sponsor has come forward, weakening the strongest relief. [premortem, structural, dialectic] — *Nature: factual assumption about third-party mobilization, plus an unverified legal question about sponsor standing.*
- **Is the penalty negotiable?** Steelman: mandatory once violation found, not a bargaining chip — the 2019 reform exists to eliminate discretionary softness. Structural and dialectic's antithesis: HCD's incentives point to a negotiated compliance win, with the penalty functioning as leverage; tetlock adds exhaustion and AG-only-enforcement gatekeeping (unverified) before liability crystallizes. [steelman, structural, dialectic, tetlock] — *Nature: mixed legal question (mandatory vs. discretionary text, unverified) and institutional-behavior prediction.*
- **Gift-clause outcome.** Tetlock estimates the taxpayer suit likely fails under *Janssen*-line deference (~27% standalone, ~30–40% adjusted for SLA coupling); the steelman constructs the transaction as an actual gift (extreme disproportion, expiring covenants, no public interest retained, no appraisal-based pricing, *Dockweiler*/*Elliott* distinguished). [tetlock, steelman] — *Nature: partly an artifact of operation design (steelman is deliberate advocacy for the disfavored pole), but the substantive split — deference vs. disproportion-plus-pretext — is a genuine legal question tetlock flags as a doctrinal gap with no on-point case.*
- **Weight of convergence built on shared unverified memory.** Within delphi, the upper cluster treated convergence-despite-incompatible-recollections as cross-validating; the lower cluster discounted it as shared-limitation convergence that cannot substitute for primary text. [delphi] — *Nature: pure epistemic risk tolerance (intra-lens persistent split, reported as such).*

## 4. Noise audit

- **delphi** is the padded lens. Its genuine signal — tight convergence at 80–84 plus four well-articulated persistent splits — is stated three times over (round-1 facilitator summary, persistent-disagreement report, seven full final-round answers), with the seven panelist answers largely restating the same three defects and the same convergence-despite-divergent-recollections observation in near-identical language. The persistent-disagreement report alone carries essentially all the lens's distinct content; the rest is volume. The lens is also heavily self-referential (panelists reasoning about the panel's own convergence), which it honestly flags but which inflates length without adding legal content.
- **tetlock**'s recomposition arithmetic (composite 93–94%, "$1.8M expected exposure") is pseudo-precision layered on subjective single-worker estimates — the lens itself warns not to quote the figures downstream, and that warning should be honored. The per-crux considerations underneath are substantive and non-duplicative.
- **dialectic** is long but earns most of its length; the main redundancy is that antithesis §§ 5–6 (cure architecture, gatekeeping) are developed at length only for the synthesis to correctly file them as out-of-scope for the proposition.
- **steelman** is not noise but is by design one-directional advocacy; its confident register ("is unlawful," "must be eliminated") should not be read as a probability estimate — tetlock's estimates on the same questions point materially softer.
- **premortem** and **structural** are dense and largely non-redundant with each other and the rest of the panel; no meaningful padding detected in either.
