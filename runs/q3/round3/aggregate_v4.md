# MERGED REPORT (lenses: delphi, tetlock, dialectic, steelman, premortem, structural, machinery)

## 1. Argument map

### Convergent (multiple independent lenses agree)

- **Core structural rule:** § 1090's prohibition runs to the contracting body itself, not just the interested member's vote, so a sitting councilmember's recusal does not by itself cure a disqualifying, non-excepted interest (Thomson v. Call; Stigall — unverified). All of Partial A's lenses treat this as the load-bearing premise; even the steelman concedes it for *direct* interests; premortem and machinery independently converge that recusal alone likely does not cure § 1090, and that treating PRA recusal and § 1090 as interchangeable is the single most consequential available error. [delphi, tetlock, dialectic, steelman, premortem, machinery]
- **PRA and § 1090 are analytically distinct regimes:** recusal is the designed, sufficient cure under §§ 87100/87103 but is necessary-not-sufficient where § 1090 applies; PRA disqualification from both votes is required regardless; PRA-style recusal fixes a PRA problem but not a § 1090 problem unless the interest falls within an express § 1091 / § 1091.5 category. [delphi, tetlock, dialectic, steelman, premortem, machinery]
- **The spouse's ~$95K salary is the central candidate interest**, imputed to Okafor via community-property principles (Fam. Code §§ 751/760; Gov. Code § 82030; *People v. Honig* — all unverified); no lens treats it as fanciful. [delphi, tetlock, dialectic, steelman, premortem, machinery]
- **Two analytically separate interests:** the board seat and the spousal salary must be analyzed leg-by-leg and require separate cures; any noninterest/remote-interest treatment of the uncompensated board seat does not reach the spousal-salary interest, and board resignation does not touch the community-property income issue. [delphi, tetlock, dialectic, steelman, premortem, machinery, structural]
- **The board seat, standing alone, is likely the easier leg** — plausibly a § 1091.5(a) noninterest for uncompensated nonprofit officers/directors (exact subsection unverified) [delphi, tetlock, dialectic, steelman]; premortem and machinery identify § 1091(b)(1) as a candidate for the uncompensated board seat *only* [premortem, machinery]. (Classification remains contested — see below.)
- **No § 1091 remote-interest or § 1091.5 noninterest category appears to cleanly cover a spouse's salaried employment** by the contracting entity. [premortem, machinery] — *Note: this proposition is contested across Partial A's lenses (steelman asserts a fitting category exists; delphi's panel split; tetlock keeps ~20% on coverage) — see Contested below.*
- **"Making" a contract under § 1090 is construed broadly** to include preliminary steps, planning, and negotiation (*Stigall*, *Thomson v. Call* — UNVERIFIED), so the RFP framework/scoring vote — and even pre-vote informal participation or staff-level criteria shaping — may independently taint the contract regardless of recusal at the award vote. [premortem, machinery, structural] — *Note: whether framework-stage recusal is legally compelled is contested among Partial A's lenses — see Contested below.*
- **"Simple" vote-only recusal is inadequate under any theory:** any statutory cure (if one exists) requires on-record disclosure before the process begins plus total non-participation — no briefings, staff contact, informal input on scoring — covering the framework stage, not just the award vote. [tetlock, dialectic, steelman]
- **Fund-walling / contract-structuring around the spouse's salary is a weak fix if used alone:** commingled nonprofit operating budgets make money fungible; § 1090 case law is said to treat the whole contract as the unit of analysis [tetlock, dialectic]; any funding "wall" between city dollars and the spouse's salary is economically illusory where Harbor Light's payroll runs through a single general operating budget ~40% fed by city contracts — fungibility defeats formal ring-fencing and supports both a § 1090 interest finding and PRA materiality [premortem, structural].
- **The only moves that retire (rather than manage) the risk are structural:** decouple the spouse's compensation/role from city-contract revenue, exclude Harbor Light from this cycle, or both; board resignation is cheap and advisable regardless of how the crux resolves. [tetlock, dialectic]
- **Consequences stack if wrong:** § 1092 voidness/avoidance, disgorgement exposure (potentially reaching Harbor Light), § 1097 criminal exposure (willfulness element), and separate FPPC/PRA enforcement. [delphi, tetlock, dialectic, steelman]
- **§ 1090 voidness is the structurally worst exposure because it is latent and long-lived:** it can be raised post-performance (or defensively, or by a future hostile council, or by the state) after the money is spent, converting a legal loss into disgorgement/restitution with no clean unwind (*Thomson v. Call* on recovery — UNVERIFIED). [premortem, structural, machinery]
- **No after-the-fact ratification cures a void § 1090 contract:** a "clean" re-vote without Okafor cannot fix it; quantum meruit/estoppel generally unavailable, subject to a possible narrow restitutionary carve-out. [dialectic, premortem, structural, machinery]
- **§ 1097 criminal exposure is enforcement-contingent:** DAs rarely charge; activation requires a whistleblower/complainant and a media-legible narrative — low probability, high severity. [premortem, structural, machinery]
- **Enforcement across all tracks (FPPC, DA, taxpayer suit) is complaint-driven and resource-constrained;** risk is triggered by external events (rival, journalist, audit, election) rather than by the merits. [structural, premortem]
- **Litigation risk is realistic, not theoretical:** losing bidders and taxpayers have standing and incentive to challenge under § 1092 / CCP § 526a (unverified). [delphi, dialectic] — *Cf. Contested below: premortem and structural disagree on the likelihood of the losing-bidder channel.*
- **The state HHAP pass-through creates a separate, layered compliance/clawback exposure** independent of local litigation, governed by the grant agreement's own conflict representations and state audit authority. [tetlock, premortem, machinery, structural] — tetlock flagged it as a gap with no independent estimate. [tetlock]
- **Rescinding or amending the 2019 liaison-seat resolution is an available structural fix** — and the one hardest to adopt (politically, per structural; procedurally with its own quorum/conflict wrinkle, per machinery). [tetlock, structural, machinery]
- **Rule of necessity is almost certainly unavailable here** — tetlock given a presumed disinterested quorum; premortem because two other bidders exist. [tetlock, premortem] — *The doctrine's trigger is contested — see below.*
- **A contemporaneous written § 1090 opinion committing to a classification before any vote blunts § 1097 willfulness exposure** even if a court later disagrees. [dialectic, steelman] — with tetlock separately flagging the willfulness element as needing verification. [tetlock] (Cf. premortem's council-resolution documentation and structural's advice-as-shield asymmetry, in Unique.)
- **A formal FPPC advice letter (§ 83114 — UNVERIFIED) or AG opinion is an available proactive offramp;** formal (not informal) FPPC advice carries follow-it immunity if facts are fully disclosed. [premortem, machinery]
- **Operational urgency and political pressure will predictably erode a hedged conservative advice into "recuse and proceed"** — the client rounds a conditional answer down to the cheapest action item. [premortem, structural]
- **The 40% budget-dependence / 60% win-rate / Director-of-Programs facts are the battleground facts** — read by most as pushing the interest toward "direct/material," but acknowledged everywhere as the pivot of the attenuation counterargument. [delphi, tetlock, dialectic, steelman]
- **Statutory-surplusage argument:** reading § 1090 to categorically bar this fact pattern would leave §§ 1091/1091.5's nonprofit-officer and employment categories with no work to do. [dialectic, steelman]
- **People v. Honig's precedential weight needs care** — its statute-of-limitations procedural history and its active-steering facts are flagged as limiting how far it carries. [tetlock, dialectic, steelman]
- **Common-law/due-process bias doctrine applies if the award is quasi-adjudicative rather than legislative** (Nasha LLC v. City of Los Angeles; BreakZone Billiards v. City of Torrance — UNVERIFIED). [dialectic, machinery]

### What looks decisive

## 4. Noise audit

- **delphi:** Substantial duplication by design — seven panelists reciting essentially the same reasoning chain (Thomson/Stigall, community-property imputation, 40%/60% facts) with per-panelist restatements; the round-trajectory tables and extended "why I didn't move to the median" meta-commentary are process narration, not legal content. Fault line 3 is explicitly non-doctrinal calibration philosophy and adds nothing a lawyer can use. That said, the persistent-disagreement report (fault lines 1–2, the Fraser-Yamor dependency flag) is genuinely high-signal and is the best-organized statement of the crux in the corpus.
- **tetlock:** Mostly substantive, but the bucket arithmetic and coherence check carry pseudo-precision — percentages composed from unverified doctrinal recollections presented to one decimal of confidence they don't have. The lens itself half-concedes this ("correlated readings of the same underlying doctrine, not independent events"). The per-crux considerations are dense but not padded.
- **dialectic:** Long, but the length is doing work — and notably self-policing: the synthesis flags the thesis's own "stacking" section (Part I.C) as advocacy framing without doctrinal support and drops it. Some duplication between thesis/antithesis consequence sections (Parts VI–VIII), which the synthesis correctly identifies as not actually contested.
- **steelman:** On-mission advocacy with no filler; its risk is not padding but rhetorical confidence — it asserts the existence and fit of a spousal-employment remote-interest category ("believed to be," "historically covered") more firmly than any lens verified, which the reader should treat as the assigned advocacy posture, not corroboration.
- **premortem:** Substantively strong and mostly non-duplicative — eight genuinely distinct failure modes with likelihood/severity rankings. Items #4 and #8 are political/communications risks rather than legal analysis, and #8 partially restates #4's dynamics; the narrative framing adds some length without adding points. Not padding, but the last quartile is thinner than the first.
- **structural:** Content-bearing but internally repetitive: the core insight — decision-makers bear the least expected cost while absent parties bear the most — is restated at least three times (sections 2, 4, and 7, and again in the drivers list). The actor table and the "what each rule produces" section are useful; the repetition inflates apparent breadth.
- **machinery:** Highest information density and lowest redundancy of its panel, but the exhaustive-inventory format includes entries of marginal relevance to the actual question (Brown Act 72-hour posting, grand jury referral, Claims Act presentation) — expected of the operation, but a reader should not mistake list length for issue-spotting weight. Its "Three Most Easily Missed" section is that panel's single most useful compression.
- **Panel-level:** One internal contradiction (§ 1097 misdemeanor vs. felony) went unflagged by any lens and is reported in Contested above. No lens produced word-salad.
- **Merge note (process, not analysis):** Two points convergent within one panel (no § 1091/§ 1091.5 category covers spousal employment [premortem, machinery]; framework vote as "making" [premortem, machinery, structural]) are simultaneously contested across the other panel's lenses; per the merge rules both are retained in Convergent with cross-references to the corresponding Contested entries rather than being silently reconciled.

## 5. Completeness addendum (second pass — recovered from lens outputs after the draft dropped it)

- Vote-necessity is irrelevant under § 1090: even where the interested member's vote was not needed for a majority or quorum, the contract is still void (Stigall; Millbrae — unverified), foreclosing the intuitive "the other four members had enough votes" defense; the antithesis's counter — that Stigall/Millbrae address only vote-necessity, not whether total non-participation under § 1091(a) suffices — is a specific verification question missing from the draft's Stigall/Millbrae entries. [dialectic]
- PRA disqualification has a second, independent basis beyond community-property income: the board seat itself as a "business position" under § 87103(d) / FPPC Reg. 18234 (unverified) — confirm whether an uncompensated nonprofit directorship qualifies; two independent PRA triggers weaken any "no financial interest at all" argument. [machinery, tetlock]
- The surplusage canon cuts both ways and the draft keeps only one direction: the thesis argues the very existence of the nonprofit-officer carve-outs in §§ 1091(b)/1091.5(a) is textual proof the Legislature treats nonprofit board membership as an "interest" within § 1090's reach — a carve-out that must be affirmatively satisfied is not the absence of an interest. [dialectic]
- Steelman's affirmative Honig distinction goes beyond the draft's "active-steering" caveat: Honig retained supervisory authority over the very subordinates who negotiated the contract, whereas Okafor is one member of a multi-member body with no unilateral appointing or supervisory power over the staff who will draft criteria, score bids, and administer the award — the structural argument counsel would deploy against a Honig analogy where recusal is genuinely comprehensive. [steelman]
- Burden allocation if challenged: the burden of establishing a curative § 1091/1091.5 category falls on the city/Harbor Light, and (per one delphi panelist, unrebutted) unresolved ambiguity about exception fit resolves *toward* disqualification under strict-construction doctrine — a litigation-posture tie-breaker sharper than the draft's generic strict-construction note. [dialectic, delphi]
- Declaratory relief (CCP § 1060) is a third proactive offramp: the city itself (or a competitor/taxpayer) can seek pre-award judicial guidance, and the synthesis names a declaratory action as what would definitively resolve the exception-fit crux absent controlling authority — omitted from the draft's offramp menu (FPPC letter, AG opinion). [dialectic, machinery]
- § 1091(a) cure mechanics beyond disclosure: the interested member must not be counted toward quorum for the item (confirm the city clerk's quorum-count method), and the remaining members must approve in good faith without counting the interested member's vote — mechanics distinct from the § 87105 "leave the room" minuting the draft retains. [machinery, steelman]
- Criminal clock: § 1097 exposure carries a presumptive 3-year felony SOL (Pen. Code § 801 — unverified), potentially extended by § 803(c) discovery-rule tolling for fraud/fiduciary-breach felonies — a latency period distinct from the civil § 1092 SOL question, which is the only limitations issue the draft develops.
