## 1. Argument map

### Convergent (multiple independent lenses agree)

- **Recusal by a sitting council member does not, by itself, cure § 1090** — the prohibition attaches to the body's capacity to contract, not to the member's vote (Thomson v. Call; Stigall — both unverified). All seven Delphi panelists treated this as the load-bearing premise; the steelman accepts it as the correct rule for *direct* interests. [delphi, tetlock, dialectic, premortem, steelman]
- **The spouse's ~$95K salary is the central candidate disqualifying interest, imputed to Okafor via community-property principles** (Fam. Code §§ 751/760 — unverified). [delphi, tetlock, dialectic, premortem, steelman]
- **The board seat and the spousal salary are two analytically distinct interests; curing one does not reach the other.** [delphi, tetlock, dialectic, steelman]
- **Okafor's uncompensated board seat likely falls within a § 1091.5-type noninterest for noncompensated nonprofit officers/directors (exact subsection unverified)** — making it the more tractable leg. [delphi, tetlock, dialectic, steelman]
- **PRA §§ 87100/87103 requires recusal from both votes, and recusal is the PRA's recognized and sufficient cure — but PRA compliance is necessary, not sufficient, where § 1090 also applies.** [delphi, tetlock, dialectic, steelman]
- **"Simple" vote-only recusal fails under every framework offered**: any statutory cure requires pre-decision disclosure on the record plus total non-participation from inception — no briefings, no staff contact, no input on scoring weights. [tetlock, dialectic, premortem, steelman]
- **The RFP framework/scoring-criteria stage is itself (or risks being) part of "making" the contract under § 1090's broad construction**, so recusal limited to the award vote does not cure earlier participation. [tetlock, dialectic, premortem]
- **Consequences of getting it wrong**: voidness/avoidance under § 1092, disgorgement even for services rendered, § 1097 criminal exposure (willfulness element — unverified), FPPC administrative penalties. [delphi, tetlock, dialectic, premortem, steelman, structural]
- **Fund-segregation "walls" are undermined by budget fungibility** — a paper wall between city dollars and the spouse's salary line in a commingled nonprofit operating budget is unlikely to survive scrutiny. [tetlock, premortem, structural, dialectic]
- **A contemporaneous, written § 1090 opinion taking a documented position before any vote materially blunts § 1097 willfulness exposure even if the classification later proves wrong.** [dialectic, steelman, premortem]
- **The rule of necessity is unavailable** (a disinterested quorum and other bidders exist). [tetlock, premortem]
- **Board resignation is cheap and advisable regardless of how the harder question resolves** (contested by steelman — see below), and **the only fix that works under *every* resolution is structural**: decouple the spouse's compensation from city-contract revenue, or Harbor Light sits this cycle out. [tetlock, dialectic, premortem, delphi]
- **No lens could verify the current text of §§ 1091(b) or 1091.5(a); every lens flagged exact subsections as unverified.** [delphi, tetlock, dialectic, steelman]

### Contested (lenses genuinely conflict)

- **Threshold classification.** One side: the spousal salary is a cognizable § 1090 "financial interest" given the 40% budget dependence, 60% win rate, and Director-level role [delphi upper cluster, tetlock (q1 at 80), dialectic-thesis]. Other side: a fixed, non-contingent salary from a majority-non-city-funded budget may fail the § 1090 threshold entirely, leaving only a PRA materiality question that recusal fully cures [delphi lower cluster, dialectic-antithesis]. *Nature: genuinely unsettled legal question — Delphi's own report says no panelist could resolve it with verified authority.*
- **Exception fit.** One side: no enumerated § 1091(b)/§ 1091.5 category reaches a spouse's salaried, director-level employment with the contracting nonprofit [delphi majority, dialectic-thesis, tetlock (q2 at 20)]. Other side: a remote-interest category historically covering fixed, non-contingent employment could reach it, making disclosure-plus-total-abstention a complete statutory cure [steelman, dialectic-antithesis, delphi minority]. *Nature: legal question compounded by a verification gap — both sides argue from unverified statutory text.*
- **Bottom line on the proposition.** Delphi converged on ~75% true (contract void/voidable despite full recusal) [delphi]; tetlock puts "recusal alone is not safe" at ~80–90% [tetlock]; dialectic-synthesis declares the fork **irreducible on this record** and refuses to resolve it by argument [dialectic]; steelman constructs a full defense of recusal-plus-documentation as lawful compliance [steelman]. *Nature: legal question overlaid with risk-tolerance — Delphi's Fault Line 3 is explicitly a calibration-philosophy split, not doctrine.*
- **Must Okafor resign the board seat?** Yes/advisable: near-unavoidable advice [tetlock], cheap and prudent regardless [dialectic, premortem]. No: the seat is a noninterest by legislative design, created by the council's own 2019 resolution for the council's own purposes [steelman]. Delphi's own panel split three ways on the seat's classification (noninterest vs. remote interest vs. uneasy fit) [delphi]. *Nature: classification is a legal question; the resignation advice is risk-tolerance.*
- **The "stacking" theory.** Dialectic-thesis argues board seat + spousal salary combine into a single incurable direct interest; dialectic-antithesis and dialectic-synthesis reject stacking as unsupported by any cited holding, and synthesis advises dropping it — the strongest case rests on the salary leg alone [dialectic]; steelman likewise analyzes each leg separately [steelman]. *Nature: legal question (an intra-dialectic fight by design, echoed independently by steelman).*
- **Weight and reading of People v. Honig (unverified).** Treated as the closest factual analog supporting disqualification [delphi, dialectic-thesis, tetlock]; distinguished as an active-steering case irrelevant to a fully walled-off official, with its limitations-reversal history discounting its precedential force [dialectic-antithesis, steelman]. *Nature: legal question about an unverified authority — and the lenses describe Honig's facts inconsistently (see noise audit).*
- **Does the spouse's Director-level seniority matter?** Yes — it defeats any rank-and-file-style exception [delphi (several panelists), tetlock]. No — the test is contingency of compensation, not title or magnitude (payroll-clerk analogy) [steelman]. Delphi's panel itself split on this [delphi]. *Nature: legal question.*
- **Public-generally exception for the framework vote.** Plainly inapplicable given three bidders [tetlock, dialectic]; a "genuinely available" argument for a facially neutral, all-bidders criteria vote, though not pressed [steelman]. *Nature: legal question.*
- **Enforcement realism.** A challenge is realistically likely — losing bidders have standing and motive under § 1092/CCP § 526a [dialectic, premortem, delphi (one panelist)]. Versus: enforcement channels are structurally dormant — losing bidders fear retaliation and need future contracts, FPPC is complaint-driven and under-resourced, and the real danger is a *latent* voidness claim raised years later by an audit, a hostile future council, or opposition research [structural]. *Nature: factual assumption about actor behavior, not doctrine.*
- **Is the framework-stage taint argument even responsive to the proposition?** Tetlock estimates 78% that criteria-setting is "making" [tetlock]; dialectic-antithesis calls this a strawman because the proposition stipulates recusal from "the RFP," which includes the framework — and dialectic-synthesis resolved it as a scope mismatch, while noting Okafor's actual question ("may I vote on the framework?") signals she may not intend full-stage recusal [dialectic]. *Nature: factual assumption about what "fully recused" covers.*

### Unique (surfaced by a single lens)

- Staff will likely pre-shape scoring criteria to the incumbent's profile *before* any vote — a taint mechanism invisible to vote-focused doctrinal analysis. [structural]
- Informal relational capital (years of liaison access, small three-bidder ecosystem) survives any formal cure; a doctrinal cure is not a behavioral cure, and no real wall is possible in a market this thin. [structural]
- Okafor's advice-seeking functions partly as a personal § 1097 mens-rea shield — protecting her far more than it protects the contract from voidness. [structural]
- The "urgency of homelessness" frame operates as social license that suppresses scrutiny by the very actors positioned to raise the conflict. [structural]
- The 2019 liaison-seat resolution is a systemic design flaw; the cleanest fix (reconsidering the liaison model for all appointees) is the politically hardest. [structural]
- The real disciplining mechanism is electoral/media, not legal — creating a private incentive to overcorrect (e.g., preemptive resignation) orthogonal to the doctrine. [structural]
- Hedged, multi-condition advice degrades in execution: councils round "recuse and resign, but that may not be enough" down to the easiest action item under deadline pressure; the fix is an ordered, dated checklist plus a council resolution documenting knowing assumption of residual risk. [premortem]
- Sequencing failure mode: board resignation *after* the framework vote comes too late to cure framework-stage taint. [premortem]
- Post-performance challenge scenario: disgorgement ordered after the $2.3M is already spent threatens Harbor Light's solvency and displaces active clients mid-contract. [premortem]
- Whistleblower-triggered § 1097 criminal referral: low probability, disproportionate political severity. [premortem]
- Two-sided political failure: advocates attack caution as sabotaging services while watchdogs attack any Harbor Light involvement as cronyism — the same memo loses both ways. [premortem, structural]
- HHAP-specific state audit/clawback exposure layered on top of local remedies — tetlock expressly flags this as a gap no crux estimated; premortem models it, including the state learning from the press reading as concealment. [tetlock, premortem]
- Whether disgorgement reaches Harbor Light as an "innocent" third party is itself unresolved and needs research. [tetlock, dialectic]
- Delegating award authority to a genuinely independent body (charter/ordinance vesting, no council ratification) as a potentially distinct path some AG guidance may treat differently from abstention. [tetlock]
- Statutory-surplusage canon: reading the combined facts as per-se direct would nullify §§ 1091/1091.5 in the recurring fact pattern they were plainly written for. [dialectic]
- Thorpe v. Long Beach Community College Dist. (unverified) flagged as possibly the closest authority on relative-employment remoteness — could cut either way. [dialectic]
- No after-the-fact ratification: a "clean" council re-vote cannot cure a contract void ab initio. [dialectic]
- Declaratory relief, or a legislative amendment adding an explicit relative-employment remote-interest category, as the only definitive resolution paths — the absence of a clean statutory answer is itself a recurring policy gap. [dialectic]
- Fraser-Yamor (unverified, with panelists disagreeing on the case name itself) as possibly direct authority that community-property theory voids a § 1090 contract via a spouse's employer; one panelist stated a meaningful part of their upward revision fails if it doesn't so hold. [delphi]
- Bucket arithmetic: roughly 20% no § 1090 problem / 16% statutory cure available / 64% structural remedy required. [tetlock]
- Okafor's fiduciary duty to Harbor Light as a dual-loyalty structural harm while shaping RFP criteria. [delphi (one panelist), dialectic]

## 2. Blind spots

- **HHAP/state pass-through exposure**: only tetlock (which admits no crux estimated it) and premortem addressed the state clawback layer; delphi, dialectic, and steelman analyzed the $2.3M as if it were purely local money. [tetlock, premortem]
- **Execution and sequencing risk**: only premortem examined *how* correct advice fails in practice — timing of resignation, informal pre-recusal contacts, hedges rounded down. Every other lens analyzed the law as if implementation were frictionless. [premortem]
- **Pre-vote, staff-level shaping of criteria**: only structural noticed that the taint mechanism may operate before any councilmember act occurs. [structural]
- **Disgorgement's reach to Harbor Light** as a third party: flagged as unresolved by two lenses, developed by none. [tetlock, dialectic]
- **Under-explored across ALL lenses**: common-law conflict doctrine — an express part of the assigned question — received only glancing citations (Nasha, BreakZone, both unverified) and no developed analysis anywhere [dialectic, steelman]. Similarly, the post-2023 FPPC regulation renumbering was flagged repeatedly [tetlock, steelman, premortem] but no lens attempted the materiality analysis under current regulations. I note these as absences only; no lens supplied the missing analysis.

## 3. For the lawyer

### Worth developing (ranked)

1. **Pinpoint-verify the current text of §§ 1091(b) and 1091.5(a)** for (i) any category reaching a spouse's fixed-salary employment with a contracting nonprofit and (ii) the noncompensated-nonprofit-officer noninterest and whether the council-designated-liaison structure satisfies it — three lenses independently identify this single research task as dispositive of the whole matter. [delphi, tetlock, dialectic, steelman]
2. **Pull Thorpe and Fraser-Yamor** — one lens flags Thorpe as possibly the closest on-point authority [dialectic]; four Delphi panelists leaned on Fraser-Yamor while disagreeing on its name and conceding their conclusion partly collapses if it doesn't hold as recalled [delphi].
3. **Search AG opinions and FPPC advice letters** on the "spouse employed by nonprofit contractor + liaison board seat" pattern — multiple lenses independently suggest guidance more specific than case law likely already exists for this common municipal fact pattern. [tetlock, dialectic, delphi]
4. **Convert the cure into an ordered, dated, documented sequence** — ethical-wall memo before the item is noticed, board resignation before framework work begins, disclosure on the record at first consideration, council resolution acknowledging residual risk — because the premortem's top failure modes are all sequencing/record failures, not analytical ones. [premortem, dialectic, tetlock]
5. **Fungibility audit before relying on any wall**: confirm whether Harbor Light even has restricted-fund accounting infrastructure; three lenses independently conclude paper segregation collapses under adversarial scrutiny. [premortem, structural, tetlock]
6. **Model the structural alternatives now** — spouse's compensation decoupled from city-contract revenue, Harbor Light sitting the cycle out, or delegation of award authority to a genuinely independent body — since multiple lenses converge that these are the only fixes robust to either resolution of the crux. [tetlock, dialectic, premortem, structural]
7. **Written pre-decision § 1090 opinion that commits to a characterization on the record** — the one step that pays off under every scenario by blunting § 1097 willfulness. [dialectic, steelman, premortem]
8. **Proactive HHAP-compliance posture with the state grantor** — flagging the conflict review in advance rather than letting the state learn from the press. [premortem, tetlock]

### What looks decisive

- **The single crux, independently named decisive by three lenses**: whether the spouse's fixed, non-contingent salary (a) clears § 1090's "financial interest in the contract" threshold at all, and if so (b) fits any enumerated § 1091(b)/§ 1091.5(a) category. Delphi's entire residual spread maps onto it (Fault Lines 1–2); tetlock names q2 the decisive crux (flipping it inverts the 64/16 outcome split); dialectic-synthesis declares it the irreducible fork and says primary-source verification of the exception catalog is "the single highest-value research task in the entire dispute." [delphi, tetlock, dialectic]
- **Secondary crux**: whether "full recusal" in fact covers the framework stage — Okafor's own phrasing suggests it may not, and framework-stage participation independently tanks any cure under every lens that examined it. [tetlock, dialectic, premortem]
- **Points that need no crux**: recusal from both votes is mandatory under the PRA regardless [delphi, tetlock, dialectic, steelman], and vote-only recusal is inadequate under every theory offered, including the steelman's own [tetlock, dialectic, premortem, steelman].
