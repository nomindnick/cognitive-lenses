## 1. Argument map

### Convergent (multiple independent lenses agree)

- The Thomson v. Call / Stigall rule (all cites UNVERIFIED) that a sitting body member's recusal does not cure § 1090 — because the prohibition runs to the contracting body, not the member's vote — is treated as the load-bearing premise by every lens; even the pro-recusal position accepts it for *direct* interests and wins only if the interest is classified remote/noninterest. [delphi, dialectic, premortem, steelman, tetlock]
- The spouse's ~$95K salary is imputable to Okafor as her own interest via community-property principles (Fam. Code §§ 751/760, UNVERIFIED), making the spousal-salary leg — not the board seat — the central candidate disqualifying interest. [delphi, dialectic, premortem, steelman, tetlock]
- The uncompensated board seat is the *more curable* leg: likely a § 1091.5-type noninterest or § 1091 remote interest (exact subsection UNVERIFIED), but any such exception is personal to the board-seat leg and does not reach the analytically distinct spousal-salary interest. [delphi, dialectic, steelman, tetlock]
- Vote-only recusal is inadequate under every theory presented: any valid path requires on-record disclosure before framework work begins plus total non-participation (no briefings, staff contact, informal input on scoring), whether framed as § 1091(a) cure mechanics or as risk hygiene. The answer to "if I simply recuse, is the city safe?" is no under both the pro- and anti-voidness positions. [dialectic, premortem, steelman, tetlock]
- The RFP framework/scoring-criteria stage is plausibly itself "making" the contract under § 1090's broad construction, so award-stage-only recusal after framework participation fails independently. [dialectic, premortem, tetlock]
- PRA §§ 87100/87103 and § 1090 are distinct regimes: recusal is the recognized and sufficient PRA cure, but PRA compliance is necessary-not-sufficient where § 1090 applies. [delphi, dialectic, steelman, tetlock]
- § 1090 exceptions are narrowly/strictly construed against the party invoking them (Stigall, Honig, Kunec — UNVERIFIED), while "financially interested" is construed broadly. [delphi, dialectic, tetlock]
- Consequences if wrong: § 1092 voidness, disgorgement even for services rendered, § 1097 criminal exposure (willfulness element), FPPC/PRA enforcement as a separate track, and challenge vectors via losing bidders/taxpayers (CCP § 526a, UNVERIFIED). [delphi, dialectic, premortem, structural, tetlock]
- A contemporaneous, written, pre-decision § 1090 opinion committing to a characterization materially blunts § 1097 willfulness exposure even if a court later disagrees. [dialectic, premortem, steelman, structural]
- Nonprofit budget fungibility undermines paper walls/ring-fencing between city dollars and the spouse's salary line — a certification-based "wall" is bookkeeping, not segregation. [dialectic, premortem, structural, tetlock]
- The revenue-dependence facts (40% city funding, 60% win rate, Director-level role, subject-matter match to the RFP) are the facts doing the most work in pushing the interest toward "direct/material." [delphi, dialectic, tetlock]
- The single question that moves the outcome most is whether any enumerated § 1091(b)/§ 1091.5(a) category covers a spouse's salaried employment with the contracting nonprofit — three lenses independently name this as the decisive/irreducible crux. [delphi, dialectic, tetlock]
- The rule of necessity is unavailable given a disinterested quorum / other realistic bidders. [premortem, tetlock]
- Directional bottom line where quantified: delphi's panel converged at median 75% that the contract would be void/voidable despite full recusal; tetlock's recomposition puts "recusal alone is not safe" at roughly 80–90% (modal outcome: structural remedy required, ~64%). [delphi, tetlock]

### Contested (lenses genuinely conflict)

- **Direct vs. remote/noninterest classification of the spousal salary.** Steelman: a fixed, non-contingent salary at a diversified nonprofit is the paradigm remote interest, curable by § 1091(a) disclosure-plus-total-non-participation (contingency, not magnitude, is the test). Delphi majority, dialectic thesis, and tetlock (q2 at 20%): no enumerated category cleanly reaches spousal employment, so the interest is direct and uncurable; dialectic's synthesis declares this fork irreducible on the current record. [steelman vs. delphi, dialectic, tetlock] — *Nature: genuinely unsettled legal question, compounded by unverified statutory text.*
- **Threshold question: is there a § 1090 "financial interest" at all?** Delphi's lower cluster and dialectic's antithesis keep alive the possibility that the attenuated salary (general operating budget, 40% minority city share, 60%-not-100% win rate) fails the threshold entirely, leaving only a PRA question that recusal cures; delphi's upper cluster and dialectic's thesis resolve the threshold toward an interest existing (Honig/Fraser-Yamor, UNVERIFIED). [delphi (internal split), dialectic (thesis vs. antithesis)] — *Nature: legal-question, plus a factual-characterization dispute over the same undisputed numbers (40%/60% read as materiality by some, attenuation by others).*
- **The "stacking" theory.** Dialectic's thesis argues board seat + spousal salary intertwine into a structural double-loyalty § 1090 exists to foreclose; dialectic's antithesis and synthesis reject stacking as unsupported by any cited holding and insist on leg-by-leg analysis (a legal-zero noninterest amplifies nothing); tetlock's q1 gestures at a "totality-of-interests" taint. [dialectic (internal), tetlock vs. dialectic-antithesis] — *Nature: legal-question (no cited authority either way).*
- **Must the board seat be resigned?** Steelman: no — it is a noninterest by design, created by the council's own 2019 resolution. Dialectic synthesis and tetlock: not strictly required if the exception fits, but cheap and advisable / "close to unavoidable advice"; premortem's assumed position urges resignation and shows failure when it comes too late. [steelman vs. dialectic, premortem, tetlock] — *Nature: primarily risk-tolerance, sitting atop the unsettled legal question.*
- **Must the spouse's employment/funding change?** Steelman: no — the statute does not condition public service on a spouse changing jobs. Dialectic synthesis and tetlock: structural decoupling (or Harbor Light sitting out the cycle) is the only move that retires the risk under either resolution of the crux; premortem's assumed position recommends restructuring. [steelman vs. dialectic, premortem, tetlock] — *Nature: legal-question (the crux itself) plus risk-tolerance.*
- **Public-generally exception (§ 87103).** Dialectic (both thesis and antithesis) and tetlock: plainly inapplicable — a competitive award among three bidders. Steelman: a genuinely available argument for the facially neutral framework vote (though mooted by prudential recusal). [dialectic, tetlock vs. steelman] — *Nature: legal-question.*
- **Likelihood of an actual challenge.** Dialectic's thesis: adversarial testing is realistically likely given three real bidders with standing and motive; premortem models a losing-bidder suit occurring. Structural: losing bidders are the structurally weakest actors — they need future contract cycles more than this win, so enforcement channels are dormant/complaint-driven, and the dangerous § 1090 channel is latent and can fire years later for unrelated reasons. [dialectic, premortem vs. structural] — *Nature: factual-assumption about enforcement economics, not doctrine.*
- **Weight of People v. Honig (UNVERIFIED).** Dialectic's thesis and delphi treat it as the closest analog for spousal-benefit § 1090 interests; dialectic's antithesis and steelman distinguish it (active steering, retained supervisory power, statute-of-limitations reversal affecting precedential force). [delphi, dialectic-thesis vs. dialectic-antithesis, steelman] — *Nature: legal-question (case reading).*
- **Whether the framework-stage taint argument even fits the tested proposition.** Dialectic's thesis relies on framework participation; dialectic's antithesis and synthesis hold this attacks a strawman because the proposition stipulates full recusal from "the RFP," which includes the framework — while noting Okafor's actual phrasing ("may I vote on the framework?") signals she may not intend full-stage recusal. [dialectic (internal)] — *Nature: scope-of-proposition dispute, resolved within the lens but relevant to how the advice question is framed.*

### Unique (surfaced by a single lens)

- The council's own 2019 resolution creating the liaison seat as affirmative support for noninterest treatment — the city itself deemed the seat institutionally valuable. [steelman]
- Payroll-clerk vs. commissioned-salesperson analogy: contingency on *this* contract, not salary magnitude, as the remote/direct test. [steelman]
- Sequencing failure as the dominant failure mode: advice phrased as parallel recommendations (recuse + resign + wall) rather than an ordered, dated checklist fails at its weakest condition; board resignation after the framework vote came too late to matter. [premortem]
- Hedged advice gets "rounded down" by a time-pressured client to the easiest action item (recusal-only), absent a documented council resolution acknowledging residual risk on the record. [premortem]
- Post-performance timing catastrophe: a challenge filed after the $2.3M is drawn down converts disgorgement from theoretical to an insolvency/client-displacement event Harbor Light cannot repay. [premortem]
- Failure to proactively notify the state grantor of a conflict under legal review reads as concealment when it surfaces via press. [premortem]
- Whistleblower-triggered § 1097 criminal referral: low probability, but reputational/governance severity (forced resignation, special election) disproportionate to legal probability. [premortem]
- Two-sided political narrative: services advocates attack the caution, good-government groups attack the award, and the same memo becomes the scandal. [premortem]
- Staff-level pre-shaping: scoring criteria drafted by staff who know the incumbent's profile may be calibrated to Harbor Light before any vote occurs — a taint mechanism invisible to vote-focused analysis. [structural]
- Informal relational capital survives formal disentanglement; in a thin three-bidder market there is no real wall around the ecosystem that produced the 60% win rate. [structural]
- Cost-allocation asymmetry: the actors who decide (Okafor, council, Harbor Light) bear the least expected downside; the spouse — the pivot of the conflict, with no vote and no formal role — bears the highest concentrated cost of the cleanest fix. [structural]
- Advice-seeking as liability-shifting: the request for counsel builds Okafor a personal mens rea shield that protects her far more than it protects the city's contract from voidness. [structural]
- Regulator incentive mapping: FPPC is complaint-driven and biased toward easy disclosure cases, not fact-intensive materiality fights; DA needs a media-legible narrative; state funder audits programmatic outputs, not conflicts. [structural]
- Adaptive-behavior forecast for each candidate rule (recusal-sufficient → more entanglement; never-cures → actual disentanglement but resisted by every concentrated interest; walls-suffice → compliance-paperwork industry). [structural]
- The 2019 liaison-seat resolution as a systemic design flaw; the cleanest fix (reconsider the liaison model generally) is politically hardest because it requires the council to admit institutional error. [structural]
- Quantified decomposition and coherence check: ~20/16/64 split across no-interest / statutory-cure / structural-remedy buckets, cross-checked against a direct 10% "recusal suffices" estimate. [tetlock]
- Rescinding or amending the 2019 liaison resolution itself as an item on the remedy menu. [tetlock]
- No-ratification point: a void § 1090 contract cannot be cured by a later "clean" re-vote without Okafor; equitable defenses and quantum meruit are generally unavailable (UNVERIFIED scope). [dialectic]
- Contract fairness is irrelevant to § 1090 voidness (City of Imperial Beach v. Bailey, UNVERIFIED). [dialectic]

## 2. Blind spots

- **HHAP clawback / state-funder exposure** — only premortem developed it (layered state recovery on top of local disgorgement, separate headline risk); tetlock expressly flagged it as a gap its own decomposition never estimated. Every other lens treated the $2.3M as if it were all local money. [premortem, tetlock]
- **Execution risk as distinct from doctrinal risk** — only premortem examined how legally correct advice fails in implementation (sequencing, informal contacts before formal recusal, unverifiable wall certifications); structural partially corroborates via the paper-compliance point. [premortem, structural]
- **Who actually enforces, and when** — only structural mapped enforcement economics; the doctrinal lenses assumed a challenger appears. The disagreement between dialectic's "likely tested adversarially" and structural's "channels dormant, risk latent for years" was engaged by no other lens. [structural, dialectic]
- **The spouse as an actor** — only structural noticed that the person who must bear the cost of the cleanest cure has no formal role, vote, or voice in the decision. [structural]
- **Staff-level criteria drafting** — only structural located taint upstream of any vote, where recusal doctrine has no purchase. [structural]
- **Under-explored across ALL lenses (absence noted, no analysis added):** common-law conflict doctrine — expressly part of the question posed — received only glancing treatment (two case names in dialectic, one phrase in premortem; steelman mentions it once as "belt-and-suspenders"); no lens analyzed it as an independent constraint. Similarly thin across all lenses: the void-vs-voidable distinction under § 1092 (one delphi panelist's Hub City cite; dialectic notes the disjunctive framing lowers the bar); whether disgorgement reaches Harbor Light as an "innocent" third party (tetlock flags it as unresolved, no lens develops it); and the current post-2023 FPPC regulation numbering/materiality thresholds (flagged repeatedly, developed nowhere).

## 4. Noise audit

- **delphi** is the noisiest output relative to information delivered. The seven panelists recite substantially the same reasoning chain (Thomson/Stigall structural rule → community-property imputation → 40%/60% nexus → no clean exception → residual attenuation doubt) seven times; the round-1-to-round-2 movement (median 72→75) is small and the per-panelist "response to panel" sections are heavily duplicative of each other. Fault Line 3 (calibration philosophy — how panelists price stacked uncertainty) is explicitly non-doctrinal and adds no legal content. The genuinely valuable delphi artifacts are compact: the two-fault-line structure (threshold vs. exception-fit), the Fraser-Yamor fragility warning, and the fact that convergence occurred among panelists who admit they cannot verify the statutory text — the panel itself flags that its agreement may reflect "shared professional priors more than a resolved authority."
- **dialectic** is long and internally repetitive — thesis and antithesis each restate the consequences machinery (voidness, disgorgement, § 1097, FPPC) that the synthesis then correctly identifies as "not actually contested," meaning a substantial fraction of both documents was adversarial theater over agreed law. The synthesis itself is the highest-density artifact in the entire pipeline.
- **premortem** is largely non-duplicative; its eight failure modes are distinct, though item #8 (press narrative) overlaps structural's media/electoral points and is more political forecasting than legal angle.
- **steelman** is on-task advocacy by design; no padding, but note it is the *only* lens supporting its position, so its apparent confidence is a product of the assigned operation, not corroboration.
- **structural** repeats the fungibility point in three places (sections 2, 6, and the drivers list) and the enforcement-economics point in two, but is otherwise the source of most of the genuinely unique material.
- **tetlock** is functional throughout; the coherence-check arithmetic is elaborate for the payoff (it confirms rather than changes the answer), and its q3/q4/q2 estimates are, as it concedes, correlated readings of the same doctrine rather than independent events — so its numerical precision should not be mistaken for independent evidence.
- No lens produced word-salad. The dominant noise pattern is cross-lens and intra-delphi repetition of the same three doctrinal pillars, which is simultaneously the strongest robustness signal in the pipeline and the bulk of its redundancy.
