## THREE MOST EASILY MISSED

1. **Recusal is not a universal cure.** PRA recusal under § 87105 fixes a PRA problem; it does **not** by itself fix a § 1090 problem unless the interest independently falls within an express § 1091 remote-interest or § 1091.5 noninterest category — confirm this before advising that "recusal + wall" makes the contract safe. Treating the two statutes as interchangeable is the single most consequential error available here.

2. **"Making" the contract likely happens twice (at least).** If the RFP framework/scoring-criteria vote and the later award vote are each independently treated as "making" the contract (per the broad *Thomson v. Call* construction), disclosure/recusal/quorum mechanics under both § 1091 and § 87105 must be repeated and separately minuted at **each** vote — not just at the final award — and any staff-level involvement by Okafor in shaping scoring criteria beforehand may also count, confirm.

3. **The spouse's employment interest and the board-seat interest are analytically separate and need separate exceptions.** Finding an available § 1091(b) or § 1091.5(a) category for the uncompensated board seat does not automatically resolve the spouse's compensated-employment interest, which appears unlisted in the standard exception categories — confirm current statutory text for any category that reaches it before assuming any combination of resignation/recusal disposes of both interests simultaneously.
