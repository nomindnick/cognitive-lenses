## 1. Argument map

### Convergent (multiple independent lenses agree)

- Recusal by a sitting member of the contracting body does not, by itself, cure a § 1090 violation; the prohibition runs to the body's capacity to contract, not the individual vote (per unverified *Thomson v. Call* / *Stigall*). Delphi's panel was 7/7 on this as the load-bearing premise. [delphi, dialectic, premortem, tetlock, machinery]
- The spouse's ~$95K salary is treated as Okafor's own candidate financial interest via community-property imputation (unverified Fam. Code §§ 751/760), not merely a derivative concern — even the pro-recusal steelman concedes an interest exists. [delphi, dialectic, premortem, steelman, tetlock, machinery]
- The board-seat interest and the spousal-salary interest are analytically separate; an exception curing the board seat does not reach the spousal salary, and each needs its own statutory home. [delphi, dialectic, steelman, tetlock, machinery]
- The uncompensated board seat is the more likely of the two interests to fit a § 1091.5(a)-type noninterest or § 1091(b) remote-interest category (exact subsection unverified across all lenses). [delphi, dialectic, steelman, tetlock, machinery]
- "Making" a contract under § 1090 reaches the RFP framework/scoring-criteria stage, so vote-only recusal at the award stage is inadequate; any cure requires disclosure on the record plus total non-participation from inception (no briefings, staff contact, or informal input). Dialectic's thesis and antithesis independently agreed on this. [dialectic, premortem, tetlock, machinery, steelman]
- PRA §§ 87100/87103 requires Okafor's recusal from both votes; recusal is the PRA's designed and sufficient cure, but PRA compliance does not resolve § 1090 — treating the two regimes as interchangeable is flagged across lenses as the central advisory error. [delphi, dialectic, tetlock, machinery, steelman, premortem]
- The "public generally" exception is a poor fit on a three-bidder competitive award (though see Contested re: the framework vote). [dialectic, tetlock, steelman]
- Consequences of getting it wrong: voidness under § 1092, disgorgement even after performance, § 1097 criminal exposure (willfulness element), FPPC administrative exposure — all citations unverified. [delphi, dialectic, premortem, tetlock, machinery, structural]
- A contemporaneous, written, pre-decision legal opinion committing to a classification materially blunts § 1097 willfulness exposure regardless of how the merits resolve. [dialectic, steelman, premortem, machinery, structural]
- Paper walls / fund ring-fencing are vulnerable to a fungibility attack: Harbor Light's commingled general operating budget means "city dollars don't pay the spouse" is bookkeeping, not economics. [premortem, structural, tetlock, dialectic]
- Losing bidders and taxpayers supply realistic challenge vehicles (§ 1092, CCP § 526a — unverified), and a void contract can be raised late, defensively, or by a future hostile actor. [delphi, dialectic, premortem, structural, machinery]
- The rule of necessity is unavailable given a disinterested quorum / two other bidders. [premortem, tetlock]
- The only fix that works under every resolution of the legal uncertainty is structural: decouple the spouse's compensation from city-contract revenue, change the spouse's role, or exclude Harbor Light from this cycle. [dialectic, tetlock, premortem]

### Contested (lenses genuinely conflict)

- **Is the spousal salary a § 1090 interest at all — and if so, direct or remote?** Delphi's lower cluster and dialectic's antithesis hold a fixed, non-contingent salary from a diversified budget (only ~40% city-funded, 60% win rate) may fall below the § 1090 threshold entirely (leaving only a PRA question recusal cures) or qualify as a curable remote interest; the steelman builds this into a full go-ahead position. Delphi's upper cluster, dialectic's thesis, premortem's assumed position, and tetlock (q2 at 20%) hold the interest is real and no enumerated exception reaches it. Dialectic's synthesis declares this fork irreducible on the current record. *Nature: genuinely unsettled legal question, compounded by a factual-characterization dispute over the same undisputed 40%/60% facts.* [delphi, dialectic, steelman, tetlock, premortem]
- **Can a properly executed recusal-plus-disclosure package lawfully deliver the contract without resignation or job change?** Steelman: yes — § 1091(a)/1091.5 mechanics, properly followed, are full statutory compliance, and reading Stigall/Thomson to swallow them creates surplusage. Delphi (median ~75% the contract is void/voidable anyway), tetlock (~80–90% recusal alone is not safe), and premortem's assumed position: no. *Nature: legal question downstream of the threshold/exception fight, inflected by risk tolerance.* [steelman, delphi, tetlock, premortem, dialectic]
- **Board-seat classification.** Delphi reported three incompatible readings: § 1091.5 noninterest (majority), § 1091 remote interest (one), and "uneasy fit" given governance-level influence (one); dialectic's thesis separately argues the noninterest premise breaks down where the entity's contract stream pays the officer's household. Steelman treats it as a clean noninterest by legislative design. *Nature: legal question (exception scope).* [delphi, dialectic, steelman]
- **The "stacking" theory.** Dialectic's thesis argues board seat + spousal salary combine into a single incurable direct interest; dialectic's antithesis and synthesis reject stacking as unsupported by any cited holding and urge leg-by-leg analysis; tetlock's q1 keeps alive a "totality-of-interests" taint possibility. *Nature: legal question — existence of authority.* [dialectic, tetlock]
- **Whether the framework vote is itself tainted "making" or a neutral legislative act.** Tetlock (q4 at 78%) and dialectic's thesis treat criteria-setting as core "making"; tetlock's own counter-consideration (Lexin-style segmentation, facially neutral criteria) and the steelman's "arguably not legally compelled / public-generally" argument cut the other way. *Nature: legal question with a factual overlay (was participation active or ministerial).* [tetlock, dialectic, steelman]
- **Must Okafor resign the board seat?** Steelman: no — resignation defeats the council's own liaison design. Dialectic synthesis: not strictly required but cheap and advisable regardless. Tetlock: "close to unavoidable advice." Premortem: yes, and *before* the framework vote or the resignation fails on timing. *Nature: predominantly risk tolerance, not doctrine.* [steelman, dialectic, tetlock, premortem]
- **Is enforcement realistically likely?** Dialectic's thesis says adversarial testing is realistically likely with three real bidders; structural says the losing bidders are the weakest party — motivated but repeat-play-deterred — and enforcement channels are dormant, complaint-driven, and slow; premortem models the suit occurring post-performance. *Nature: factual assumption about enforcement behavior, not law.* [dialectic, structural, premortem]

[premortem, machinery, tetlock]

The doctrinal lenses treat cures as static options, not as processes that fail at their weakest condition. [premortem, structural]
- **Who bears the unwind** — Harbor Light's restitution exposure as a non-culpable party, insolvency risk, and client displacement — caught by premortem and machinery (and flagged as unresolved by tetlock q6); absent from the doctrinal lenses' remedy discussions. [premortem, machinery, tetlock]
- **Enforcement-realism vs. doctrine gap:** structural alone maps why nobody in the room is incentivized to resolve the question conservatively, and why the latent § 1090 voidness channel is dangerous precisely because it needs no prompt challenger. [structural]
- **Under-explored across ALL lenses (absence noted, no analysis added):** the question expressly asked about *common-law conflict doctrine*, and no lens developed it — dialectic cites *Nasha*/*BreakZone* in passing and machinery lists it as a confirm-item track; none analyzed it. ## 5. Completeness addendum (second pass — recovered from lens outputs after the draft dropped it)

- A § 1090 violation cannot be cured by after-the-fact ratification or a "clean" re-vote of the disinterested majority once the taint attached, and the contractor's estoppel/quantum-meruit recovery is generally barred (verify scope of any narrow restitutionary carve-out) — the draft's consequences bullet omits that the most intuitive fix (re-vote) is unavailable. [dialectic]
- § 1097 conviction carries forfeiture of office and disqualification from future public office (verify whether forfeiture is automatic or requires a separate proceeding); the lenses also conflict on offense classification — machinery says felony, premortem and tetlock say misdemeanor — a discrepancy the draft neither reports nor flags for verification. [machinery, dialectic, premortem, tetlock]
- The § 1091(a) cure has specific mechanics beyond "disclosure plus non-participation": disclosure entered in the official record *before* each contract-making act, the interested member not counted toward quorum for the item, and good-faith approval by the remaining members — and these must be repeated and separately minuted at **both** the framework vote and the award vote. [machinery, steelman]
- Complete delegation of final award authority to a genuinely independent decision-maker (charter/ordinance vesting in a staff officer or separate commission with **no** council ratification step) is treated by some AG guidance as materially different from abstention — while segmented/"independent panel" workarounds are viewed skeptically where the council retains ultimate approval; adjacent structuring options (blind scoring, multi-year contracts predating any member's tenure, RFP redesign to reduce winner-take-all dynamics) were also dropped. [tetlock, premortem, structural]
- Pre-award declaratory relief (CCP § 1060) is available to the city itself as a vehicle to resolve the direct-vs-remote classification crux judicially before any award — the draft lists § 1060 only as a cite to verify, not as an affirmative offramp. [dialectic, machinery]
- Proactively notify/coordinate with the HHAP administering state agency that a conflict question is under legal review — premortem's clawback scenario turns on the state learning from the press, which "read as concealment" and compounded the clawback and future-allocation damage. [premortem]
- Prepare a plain-language public-facing explanation before the memo (or its existence) leaks, plus contemporaneous written instructions to Okafor herself to avoid all informal contact — the two-sided-blowback and whistleblower-referral failure modes both trace to a communications/documentary-hygiene vacuum, and the draft recovers the failure modes but not the mitigations. [premortem]
- An internal ethical wall at Harbor Light insulating the spouse from bid preparation — not required by § 1090 doctrine but strengthens the record against common-law bias and appearance-of-impropriety challenges (Nasha/BreakZone line). [dialectic]
- Confirm whether the RFP scoring/award is legislative or quasi-adjudicative in character — this threshold characterization controls both whether due-process/bias doctrine applies and the correct writ vehicle (CCP § 1085 vs. § 1094.5). [machinery]
- Insurance checks dropped from the who-pays analysis: the city's liability/JPA risk-pool policy exclusions for intentional conflict-of-interest or criminal conduct, Harbor Light's D&O coverage of Okafor's board-seat exposure, and the specific §§ 995.2/996.4 grounds for refusing defense/indemnity (the draft cites only "§§ 995 et seq." generically). [machinery]
- Open disgorgement-scope question: whether recoverable "compensation" reaches the spouse's salary or only payments to the officer/contractor (People v. Sperl — verify pinpoint). [machinery]
- *Carson Redevelopment Agency v. Padilla* — analogized by tetlock's q6 alongside Honig and Lexin but missing from the draft's must-verify case list. [tetlock]
- Unwinding the liaison designation requires formal council rescission of the 2019 resolution — a vote with its own conflict/quorum wrinkle that must be timed relative to the RFP calendar; the draft recovers the "rescind the liaison model" idea but not the rescission-vote mechanics. [machinery, structural]
