# Statutory Machinery Inventory — Piedra Blanca Franchise / CPRA / Brown Act Matter

**Threshold caveat to flag for the attorney:** The CPRA was renumbered without (intended) substantive change, effective 1/1/2023, from Gov. Code §§ 6250–6270 to §§ 7920.000–7930.215 (Legislative Counsel nonsubstantive reorganization, AB 2811/SB 1439 process). All CPRA citations below are given in current numbering with the pre-2023 section noted parenthetically; **every section number must be independently confirmed against current code** before calendaring or filing.

## Three Items Most Likely to Be Missed

1. **The Brown Act cure-and-correct clock (§ 54960.1(b)) has no clean trigger date on these facts** — because the alleged violation is a pattern of serial deliberation with no discrete recorded "action" until (arguably) the RFP release or the eventual award vote, whoever calendars this needs to affirmatively decide and document which date starts the 90-day (or 30-day) period, or risk the window lapsing unnoticed while attention is on the CPRA suit.

2. **Any internal bid-protest deadline in the RFP itself** — these are typically 5–10 calendar days from notice of award, live in a document nobody has pulled yet in this inventory, and are easy to lose track of entirely because the visible dispute (CPRA, Brown Act) is running on a completely different, longer statutory clock.

3. **The exact timing of continued Signal auto-deletion relative to notice of the CPRA request/preservation demand** — this is not a filing deadline but a factual precondition that determines whether Gov. Code § 6200, Penal Code § 135, and civil spoliation doctrine are even in play; if nobody nails down the day-by-day chronology now, the evidentiary basis for any of those remedies may become impossible to reconstruct later since the very medium at issue is self-destructing.
