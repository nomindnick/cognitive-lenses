## 1. Argument map

### Convergent (multiple independent lenses agree)

- The Signal content (franchise terms, diversion targets, "what council will realistically accept," RFP timing) very likely qualifies as "public record" material under *San Jose*'s content-based, medium-neutral test; several lenses independently call this the strongest prong (delphi: 7/7 panelists, Theme A; tetlock: P=82; dialectic thesis and synthesis both land here; premortem's assumed counsel position concedes it; steelman states it as the conventional view before arguing against it). [delphi, dialectic, premortem, tetlock, steelman]
- Participation by three of five councilmembers — a voting majority — strengthens the public-business nexus beyond *San Jose*'s own single-official facts. [delphi, dialectic, tetlock]
- A city-systems-only search, against a request expressly covering "any platform or account," likely fails the CPRA's reasonable-search/duty-to-assist standard; the *San Jose* protocol (direct custodians to search personal accounts and certify) was never followed. [delphi, dialectic, premortem, tetlock]
- The ~40 surviving screenshots supply an independent violation theory: "no responsive records" was arguably factually false when given, regardless of how the harder search-methodology doctrine resolves (delphi: 6/7 panelists; premortem escalates it to a "knowing certification" risk). [delphi, dialectic, premortem, tetlock]
- The § 34090 retention theory is the weakest claim in the set: the statute presupposes city custody, extension to personal-device ephemeral messaging is unsettled/novel, and even the affirmative case self-describes it as an extension rather than existing law (tetlock: P=28; dialectic's own synthesis sided against it). [dialectic, steelman, tetlock, premortem, machinery]
- Realistic CPRA remedies are declaratory/injunctive relief — which does not require the records to still exist — plus near-mandatory fee-shifting to a prevailing/catalyst plaintiff; the Act has no damages provision. [delphi, dialectic, tetlock, steelman, structural, machinery]
- Criminal referral theories (Gov. Code §§ 6200–6201, § 54959) face serious willfulness/"required to be kept" problems, aggravated by the auto-delete setting having been configured by a private consultant, not a city actor. [dialectic, tetlock, steelman, structural, machinery]
- The Brown Act track is where the real money is: § 54960.1 voidability reaches the $180M award itself, an exposure category the CPRA claim cannot touch (tetlock names it a "non-subordinate decisive question"; premortem's two top failure modes run through it; structural calls the award "the real prize"). [tetlock, structural, premortem, dialectic, machinery]
- Brown Act issues should be kept procedurally separate from the CPRA action, and cure-and-correct timing under § 54960.1 is a live, possibly fatal constraint on that track. [dialectic, steelman, machinery, tetlock, structural]
- Immediate cure steps converge across adversarial and defensive framings alike: supplemental CPRA response; direct the three members to search and certify; litigation hold and forensic preservation of the screenshots and any cloud backups; produce or log the screenshots. [dialectic, premortem, steelman, structural, machinery]
- Forward-looking policy converges too: bar or capture ephemeral apps for city business, fold personal-device communications into the retention schedule, add Brown Act group-chat training — with the shared caveat that post-hoc adoption cures nothing retroactively. [dialectic, premortem, steelman, structural, tetlock]
- The exact day-by-day chronology (deletion cadence vs. CPRA request vs. any preservation-duty date) is outcome-determinative for spoliation, Pen. Code § 135, and § 6200 theories — and the evidence is self-destructing. [machinery, dialectic, tetlock, premortem]
- A verification gap sits under key authorities: the AB 700 personal-account provision was cited with mutually inconsistent section/chapter numbers, the 2018 *San Jose* remand decision's holding is disputed, and all CPRA sections need post-2023-recodification confirmation. [delphi, dialectic, machinery, tetlock, steelman]
- A financial-interest diligence track (§ 1090 / Political Reform Act / FPPC / Levine Act § 84308) is flagged as separate exposure and as the only lever that reaches GreenCycle and its consultant directly. [premortem, structural, machinery, dialectic]
- Bottom-line calibration roughly converges: delphi's panel landed at 76–79 that the proposition holds; tetlock's recomposition yields ~0.69 for a meaningful CPRA violation and ~0.93 that *some* adverse transparency finding (CPRA or Brown Act) lands. [delphi, tetlock]

### Contested (lenses genuinely conflict)

- **Public-record status of the full corpus.** Majority: the content-based test covers it and platform/host identity is immaterial — making host-identity dispositive would recreate the loophole *San Jose* closed [delphi (majority), dialectic:thesis, dialectic:synthesis, tetlock]. Contra: "prepared, owned, used, or retained *by the agency*" fails where a bidder's consultant created, administered, and destroyed the channel — officials were lobbying targets, not principals, and a participant-by-participant sort is required [steelman, dialectic:antithesis, delphi (fault line 4 minority)]. *Nature: legal-question — all sides concede no on-point authority on third-party ephemeral channels.*
- **What triggers the duty to inquire of individual custodians.** Request's "any platform or account" language alone suffices [delphi (one camp), dialectic:thesis, dialectic:synthesis (self-flagged as the synthesizer's own construction, not settled law), tetlock] vs. actual/constructive knowledge of the group is required, preserving a good-faith-search defense [delphi (other camp), dialectic:antithesis, steelman]. *Nature: legal-question — expressly unsettled post-San Jose per both delphi and dialectic.*
- **Was the original response a violation, or merely incomplete pending supplementation?** Objectively false when given, so a violation independent of search diligence [delphi (majority), premortem] vs. reasonableness judged on then-known facts; CPRA is built around supplementation, not a one-shot trap [dialectic:antithesis, steelman, delphi (minority)]. *Nature: legal-question, plus one embedded factual assumption — that the screenshots existed at response time, which delphi flags as inferred, not stipulated.*
- **Adverse-inference / spoliation viability.** Timing defeats it — deletions ran on a routine cycle before any request or reasonably anticipated litigation [dialectic:antithesis, dialectic:synthesis, tetlock, steelman] vs. a court finds the preservation duty arose no later than the award loss/demand letter, and the adverse inference is precisely what unwinds the franchise award [premortem]. *Nature: factual-assumption (when the duty attached) plus risk-tolerance.*
- **Brown Act fit.** Paradigm (or easier-than-paradigm) serial meeting: majority in one continuous substantive thread [dialectic:thesis, tetlock, premortem] vs. consultant is a lobbyist broadcasting a pitch, not the members' intermediary building concurrence, and the doctrine may not fit a single continuous chat at all [steelman, dialectic:antithesis]. *Nature: legal-question plus factual (did members exchange positions or passively receive).*
- **Scope of any public-records holding.** Categorical, class-wide characterization — status attaches at creation [delphi (one camp), dialectic:thesis, tetlock] vs. confined to the ~40 surviving screenshots, with the deleted bulk routed to a retention/spoliation lane [delphi (fault line 3 camp), dialectic:antithesis]. *Nature: proposition-interpretation plus legal-question.*
- **Settlement/containment strategy.** Negotiated settlement is the modal, rational outcome for a repeat-player defendant [structural] vs. the settle-quietly strategy fails badly — the plaintiff refuses to moot, an adverse published precedent issues, and the containment file itself becomes discoverable and becomes the story [premortem]. *Nature: factual-assumption about counterparty/amicus incentives plus risk-tolerance.*
- **§ 34090's reach.** Functional, content-based reading — officials cannot manufacture a retention loophole by choice of technology [dialectic:thesis] vs. custody-presupposing text; any duty runs to individuals, and the fix is prospective policy, not a retroactive violation finding [dialectic:antithesis, dialectic:synthesis, steelman, tetlock]. *Nature: legal-question.*

Seven panelists restate the same three arguments (content test, "any platform" language, screenshot falsity); round-two movement was trivial (median unchanged at 78, spread narrowing 76–79); the full-panelist detail block substantially repeats the facilitator summary; and much of the "response to panel" text is meta-commentary justifying non-movement. Because they were independent, this duplication doubles as evidence that the contrarian case has a stable best form; but as reading material it is redundant.

- **tetlock**'s recomposition contains invented-precision arithmetic (e.g., P(B|A)=0.80, P(B|¬A)=0.20) that the lens itself flags as its own combination judgment, and the headline ~0.93 figure rests on an independence assumption it flags but does not defend.
- **structural**'s closing "non-doctrinal drivers" section restates points already made in its §§ 1–7 — mild padding, but the underlying content is distinctive.
- **machinery** is the lowest-noise output: dense, non-duplicative, and its "three items most likely to be missed" earns its space.
- **dialectic** is long but each component does distinct work; one caution — its synthesis introduces a "request-scope trigger" rule the synthesizer expressly labels as its own construction rather than settled law, which is honestly flagged but should not be mistaken for lens-surfaced authority.
