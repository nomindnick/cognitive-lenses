## 1. Argument map

### Convergent (multiple independent lenses agree)

- *City of San Jose*'s content-based, medium-neutral test likely reaches the Signal content: franchise terms, diversion targets, "what council will realistically accept," and RFP timing are core public business, and several lenses independently call this a *stronger* fact pattern than *San Jose* itself. [delphi, dialectic, premortem, tetlock]
- Participation by three of five members — a voting majority — strengthens the public-business nexus beyond *San Jose*'s individual-official facts. [delphi, dialectic, tetlock]
- Public-record status attaches at creation; later destruction goes to remedy, not classification, and declaratory relief does not require the records to presently exist. [delphi, dialectic, tetlock]
- A city-systems-only search, with zero inquiry to the three named councilmembers, against a request expressly covering "any platform or account," is likely an inadequate search. [delphi, dialectic, premortem, tetlock]
- The ~40 surviving screenshots are an independent anchor: "no responsive records" was arguably factually inaccurate the moment it was given, separate from the harder deleted-messages questions, and the screenshots are the one concrete recoverable record set. [delphi, dialectic, premortem, tetlock]
- The § 34090 retention theory is the weakest claim in the set — the statute presupposes city custody of the record, the affirmative case itself flags the theory as an "extension" with no case on point, and tetlock's panel priced it at only 28%. [dialectic, steelman, tetlock]
- Realistic remedy menu: declaratory relief, a compelled supplemental custodian-level search, production of the screenshots, and one-way fee-shifting (including on a catalyst theory) — no damages, and nothing resurrects the deleted content. [delphi, dialectic, steelman, structural, tetlock]
- Criminal-referral theories (§§ 6200–6201, § 54959) face serious willfulness/mens-rea and custody-of-destruction problems (the consultant, not any official, set the auto-delete); "flag for independent evaluation," not a likely court-ordered outcome. [dialectic, steelman, tetlock]
- Brown Act exposure is serious on these facts, and it — not the CPRA claim — is where the largest dollar exposure lives, via potential § 54960.1 voidability of the $180M award; but the cure-and-correct clock/tolling question clouds that remedy. [dialectic, premortem, structural, tetlock]
- Classification and disclosability are distinct: even if the writings are records, preliminary-draft/deliberative-process exemptions may shield some content, requiring category-by-category (likely in camera) review rather than blanket production. [delphi, dialectic, premortem, steelman]
- The doctrinal trigger for the duty to inquire of individual officials — broad request language alone vs. actual/constructive agency knowledge — is genuinely unsettled post-*San Jose*, with no verified controlling authority; the dialectic synthesis expressly flags its own "request-scope-trigger" rule as constructed, not located in a case. [delphi, dialectic, steelman, tetlock]
- Immediate cure steps converge across adversarial postures: issue a supplemental CPRA response, direct the three members to search personal devices/accounts with agency-supplied terms and certify results, and impose an immediate litigation hold/preservation of the screenshots. [dialectic, premortem, steelman, structural]
- Forward-looking fix is a policy/ordinance matter, not a retroactive violation finding: bar or capture ephemeral-app communications on city business, forwarding/archival duties, retention-schedule amendment, and Brown Act group-chat training. [dialectic, premortem, steelman, structural, tetlock]
- The clerk has no practical or legal power to compel searches of co-equal elected officials' devices — a gap between the doctrinal duty and administrative capacity that limits both liability theories and the strength of any future policy. [dialectic, structural]

### Contested (lenses genuinely conflict)

- **Classification of the corpus.** One side: content controls; officials' three-month active engagement satisfies "used"; host identity is immaterial and treating it otherwise recreates the loophole *San Jose* closed [delphi (majority), dialectic:thesis, dialectic:synthesis, tetlock]. Other side: the channel was prepared, owned, and destroyed by a bidder's consultant — a private lobbying/capture channel in which officials were targets, not principals — and passive receipt is not agency "use"; a participant-by-participant, message-by-message sort defeats the categorical proposition [steelman, dialectic:antithesis, delphi (minority, fault line 4)]. *Nature: legal-question (scope of "prepared, owned, used, or retained" for third-party-hosted ephemeral channels — no published authority located by any lens).*
- **Did the original response "violate the Act"?** One side: violation — either because the request's own scope required forwarding it to the five named individuals regardless of the clerk's knowledge, or because the response was objectively false given the extant screenshots [delphi (majority), dialectic:thesis, dialectic:synthesis, tetlock]. Other side: reasonableness is judged on what was knowable at the time; with no notice of the Signal group, the response was an accurate snapshot, and CPRA is built around supplementation, not a one-shot trap [steelman, dialectic:antithesis, delphi (minority, Theme G)]. *Nature: legal-question (trigger standard) plus a factual assumption — delphi flags that the screenshots' existence at the response date is inferred, not stipulated.*
- **Is response falsity a standalone violation?** One side: objective falsity establishes a violation independent of search diligence; other side: a court may fold everything into a fact-bound search-adequacy inquiry crediting good faith [delphi, fault line 2 — both camps within the panel]. *Nature: legal-question.*
- **Scope of any records holding.** One side: a court characterizes the class categorically, as *San Jose* announced a principle [delphi (some panelists), dialectic:synthesis]. Other side: the holding may be confined to the ~40 surviving screenshots, routing the deleted bulk into a retention/spoliation lane and only partially validating the proposition [delphi (other panelists, fault line 3), dialectic:antithesis]. *Nature: proposition-interpretation plus unsettled law.*
- **Adverse inference / spoliation in the franchise litigation.** One side: a preservation duty plausibly arose no later than the award loss/demand letter; an adverse-inference instruction is the highest-ranked failure mode and threatens rescission of the award [premortem, dialectic:thesis]. Other side: the auto-delete ran on its ordinary cycle before any request or reasonably anticipated litigation, defeating the anticipated-litigation nexus absent evidence the cadence changed post-duty [dialectic:antithesis, dialectic:synthesis, steelman, tetlock]. *Nature: factual assumption (when the duty arose relative to the deletion cadence) plus risk-tolerance.*
- **Brown Act violation strength.** One side: a majority in one continuous substantive chat is a textbook — arguably easier-than-classic — serial-meeting pattern (tetlock P=78) [dialectic:thesis, premortem, tetlock]. Other side: under the *Wolfe*-style distinction, a consultant broadcasting a client's pitch is lobbying, not an intermediary used by members to develop collective concurrence; whether a single asynchronous disappearing chat is a "meeting" at all is untested, and the claim may be time-barred [steelman, dialectic:antithesis]. *Nature: legal-question plus factual assumption (did members exchange positions with each other or passively receive).*
- **Brown Act ↔ CPRA interaction.** Corroboration that the content was substantive public business [dialectic:thesis] vs. an impermissible bootstrap of a distinct, possibly untimely claim [dialectic:antithesis, steelman], with the synthesis permitting only narrow evidentiary use and procedural severance [dialectic:synthesis]. *Nature: legal-question.*
- **Containment/settlement strategy.** Settle-and-moot is both the likely counsel recommendation and the structurally modal outcome [premortem (assumed position), structural] vs. the premortem retrospective's projection that mooting fails, produces worse published statewide precedent, and the "quiet" containment file itself becomes discoverable and becomes the story [premortem (retrospective)]. *Nature: risk-tolerance / strategic prediction, not doctrine.*

[dialectic, tetlock]

[premortem, tetlock]

- The exemption fight over the screenshots cuts both ways on ephemerality, and neither argument made the draft: the auto-delete design itself arguably satisfies the "not retained in the ordinary course of business" prong of the preliminary-drafts exemption (city side); conversely, the councilmember's deliberate preservation of these specific messages, plus the unusually high § 7922.000/§ 6255 public interest in exposing bidder capture (*Register Div.*), cuts against withholding (requester side). [steelman]

- Steelman's reductio on classification: if participation alone suffices, any lobbyist, consultant, or bidder can unilaterally manufacture a "public record" — and a retention obligation — merely by adding a councilmember to a group chat the official never requested and cannot preserve. [steelman]
